## 1. Chest

### 1.1 Bench Press & Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Primary Target | Secondary Target |
|-----------|------------|-----------|----------------|------------------|
| Flat Barbell Bench Press | Intermediate | Barbell + rack | Mid chest (sternal pec major) | Triceps, front delt |
| Incline Barbell Bench Press | Intermediate | Barbell + rack | Upper chest (clavicular pec major) | Front delt, triceps |
| Decline Barbell Bench Press | Advanced | Barbell + rack | Lower chest | Triceps |
| Dumbbell Bench Press | Intermediate | Dumbbells | Mid chest (greater ROM) | Triceps, stabilizers |
| Incline Dumbbell Press | Intermediate | Dumbbells | Upper chest | Front delt |
| Close-Grip Bench Press | Advanced | Barbell | Triceps (also hits inner chest) | Mid chest |
| Wide-Grip Bench Press | Intermediate | Barbell | Outer chest | Front delt (less triceps) |
| Floor Press | Intermediate | Barbell or dumbbells | Mid chest (limited ROM) | Triceps (safer for shoulders) |
| Spoto Press | Advanced | Barbell | Mid chest (paused 1-2" above chest) | Triceps, explosive drive |
| Reverse-Grip Bench Press | Advanced | Barbell | Upper chest (uncommon) | Triceps |

**Coaching Cues**:
- **Setup**: Retract and depress scapulae — "pinch a pencil between your shoulder blades." Drive feet into the floor. Arch is acceptable (powerlifting) but lumbar must stay supported.
- **Descent**: Bar touches lower sternum (nipple line). Elbows at ~75° — not flared (shoulder impingement risk) nor tucked (triceps dominance).
- **Ascent**: Drive bar back toward the rack — not straight up. Exhale through the sticking point.

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Flared elbows (90°) | Tuck to ~75°; touch lower on chest |
| Bouncing off chest | Pause on all reps outside max attempts |
| Lifting shoulders off bench | Retract scapulae HARD — keep them pinned |
| Grip too narrow/wide | Knurling rings = standard grip width |
| Feet not planted | Drive through heels, not toes |

**Progressions**:
```
Beginner:   Dumbbell Bench Press (safer groove) → Barbell Bench Press
Intermediate: Spoto Press (pause work) → Slingshot bench (overload)
Advanced:   Competition bench → Board press → Floor press (lockout)
```

**Programming**:
- **Strength (3-5 reps)**: 3-5 sets, 80-90% 1RM, 3-5min rest
- **Hypertrophy (8-12 reps)**: 3-4 sets, 65-80% 1RM, 60-90s rest
- **Endurance (15-20 reps)**: 2-3 sets, 50-60% 1RM, 30-45s rest
- **Frequency**: 1-2x/week per variation; rotate incline/flat/decline across mesocycles

### 1.2 Flye Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Target | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Dumbbell Flat Flye | Intermediate | Dumbbells | Mid chest adduction | Greater stretch at bottom |
| Incline Dumbbell Flye | Intermediate | Dumbbells | Upper chest | Keep elbows slightly bent |
| Cable Flye (low to high) | Intermediate | Cable crossover | Upper chest | Constant tension |
| Cable Flye (high to low) | Intermediate | Cable crossover | Lower chest | Cross hands at finish |
| Pec Deck Flye | Beginner | Machine | Mid chest | Fixed path, stable |
| Decline Dumbbell Flye | Advanced | Dumbbells | Lower chest | High stretch risk |
| Resistance Band Flye | Beginner | Bands | General chest | Great for warm-ups |
| Straight-Arm Pullover | Intermediate | Dumbbell or bar | Chest + lats | Hybrid chest/lat movement |

**Coaching Cues**: "Hug a tree" at the top. Maintain ~15° elbow bend — do NOT change the angle during the rep. Control the eccentric (3-4s). Stretch at the bottom but don't bounce.

**Common Mistake**: Dropping dumbbells too deep (shoulder strain). Stop when arms are parallel to floor.

## 2. Back

### 2.1 Row Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Primary Target | Secondary Target |
|-----------|------------|-----------|----------------|------------------|
| Barbell Bent-Over Row | Intermediate | Barbell | Mid back (lats, rhomboids) | Erector spinae, biceps |
| Pendlay Row | Advanced | Barbell | Upper back (explosive from floor) | Lats, scapular retractors |
| One-Arm Dumbbell Row | Intermediate | Dumbbell | Lats (unilateral) | Rhomboids, biceps |
| T-Bar Row | Intermediate | T-bar / landmine | Mid back | Rhomboids, rear delts |
| Seated Cable Row | Beginner | Cable machine | Mid back | Rhomboids, biceps |
| Chest-Supported Row | Beginner | Bench + dumbbells | Mid back (no lower back stress) | Rhomboids, rear delts |
| Meadows Row | Advanced | Landmine | Lats (stretch bias) | Rear delts |
| Inverted Row | Beginner | Barbell in rack | Upper back | Scapular retraction |
| Kroc Row | Intermediate | Heavy dumbbell | Lats (high-rep, cheating) | Grip, biceps |
| Single-Arm Cable Row | Intermediate | Cable | Lats (constant tension) | Rotational stability |

**Coaching Cues**:
- **Bent-Over Row**: Hinge at hips, torso ~45° to floor. Bar starts just below knees. Pull to lower sternum. Squeeze shoulder blades for 1s at top. DO NOT jerk the weight — control the eccentric.
- **Pendlay Row**: Start from a dead stop on the floor every rep. Explosive from floor to sternum. Torso parallel to floor.
- **One-Arm Row**: Knee and same-side hand on bench. Pull elbow past torso. Do NOT rotate the torso — keep hips square.

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Ego-lifting / excessive body swing | Drop weight — control the eccentric |
| Pulling to the belly button | Pull to STERNUM / lower chest |
| Rowing with arms only | Initiate by retracting scapula FIRST |
| Rounded lower back | Brace core — maintain neutral spine |
| Short ROM | Full extension at bottom, full squeeze at top |

**Progressions**:
```
Beginner:   Cable Row → Chest-Supported Row → Inverted Row
Intermediate: Dumbbell Row → Barbell Row → T-Bar Row
Advanced:   Pendlay Row → Meadows Row → Heavy Kroc Rows
```

**Programming**: Back responds well to volume. 12-20 sets/week across all pulling. Rows train horizontally — pair with vertical pulls (pull-ups/pulldowns). Use rows 2x/week.

### 2.2 Pull-Up & Pulldown Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Target | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Lat Pulldown (wide pronated) | Beginner | Cable machine | Lats (width) | Great for building pull-up strength |
| Neutral-Grip Lat Pulldown | Beginner | Cable machine | Lower lats | Strongest grip position |
| Reverse-Grip Pulldown | Intermediate | Cable machine | Lower lats, biceps | Underhand grip |
| Straight-Arm Pulldown | Beginner | Cable | Lats (isolation) | Scapular movement only |
| Banded Pull-Up | Intermediate | Pull-up bar + bands | Lats (regression) | Band reduces bodyweight |
| Standard Pull-Up | Intermediate | Pull-up bar | Lats, biceps | Pronated, shoulder-width |
| Chin-Up | Intermediate | Pull-up bar | Lats, biceps (more) | Supinated grip |
| Wide-Grip Pull-Up | Advanced | Pull-up bar | Upper lats (width) | Harder ROM |
| Commando / Rock Climb Pull-Up | Advanced | Pull-up bar | Brachialis, lats | Side-facing, alternating |
| L-Sit Pull-Up | Advanced | Pull-up bar | Lats, abs | L-sit position throughout |
| Archer Pull-Up | Elite | Pull-up bar | Unilateral lats | One arm pulls, other straight |
| Muscle-Up | Elite | Pull-up bar / rings | Full back + chest + triceps | Transition from pull to dip |
| Weighted Pull-Up | Advanced | Pull-up bar + belt | Max strength lats | Add 5-45 lbs |

**Coaching Cues**:
- **Lat Pulldown**: Lean back ~15°. Pull bar to upper chest. DO NOT pull behind neck (shoulder impingement risk). Control the tempo — slow eccentric (3s).
- **Pull-Up**: Dead hang at bottom (no kipping unless specified). Drive elbows DOWN and BACK. Chin over bar. Avoid "craning" neck to clear the bar.
- **Chin-Up**: Palms facing you. Same mechanics but allows more load. Easier for most due to biceps assistance.

**Scapular Control Drill**: Perform "scapular pull-ups" — hang, retract scapulae to raise body 2-3" without bending elbows. This teaches the initial pull.

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Pulling behind neck | Always pull bar to CHEST / front |
| Kipping when strict is prescribed | Control the swing — cross ankles |
| Incomplete extension at top | Dead hang each rep |
| Craning neck to complete rep | Chest to bar instead of chin over bar |
| Using momentum for pulldowns | Controlled eccentric — no leaning |

**Progression Path**:
```
Beginner:   Lat Pulldown → Banded Pull-Up → Negative Pull-Ups (5s eccentric) → Pull-Up
Intermediate: Pull-Up → Chin-Up → Wide-Grip Pull-Up → Weighted Pull-Up (5-10 lbs)
Advanced:   Weighted Pull-Up (heavy) → L-Sit Pull-Up → Archer Pull-Up → Muscle-Up
```

**Programming**: Pull-ups respond to frequency. "Grease the groove" — low-volume sets throughout the day. For strength: 3-5 sets of 3-5 (weighted). For hypertrophy: 3-4 sets of 8-12. For endurance: as many quality reps as possible, multiple sets.

## 3. Shoulders

### 3.1 Press Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Primary Target | Secondary Target |
|-----------|------------|-----------|----------------|------------------|
| Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press | Beginner | Dumbbells | All delts | Triceps (minor) |
| Standing Barbell Overhead Press | Intermediate | Barbell | All delts, core | Triceps |
| Seated Barbell Press (Military Press) | Intermediate | Barbell + bench | All delts | Triceps |
| Arnold Press | Intermediate | Dumbbells | All delts (rotational) | Rotator cuff |
| Push Press | Advanced | Barbell | All delts (explosive) | Leg drive, triceps |
| Single-Arm Dumbbell Press | Intermediate | Dumbbell | Core stability, delts | Anti-rotation |
| Landmine Press | Beginner | Barbell in landmine | Front delt (shoulder-safe) | Core |
| Half-Kneeling Press | Intermediate | Dumbbell or bar | Core stability, delts | Glute activation |
| Behind-the-Neck Press | Advanced | Barbell | Rear delts (caution required) | Traps |
| Machine Shoulder Press | Beginner | Machine | Front/mid delts | Safe for beginners |

**Coaching Cues**:
- **Standing OHP**: Feet hip-width, brace core. Bar starts at collarbone (in clean rack position or from rack). Press bar directly overhead — keep bar path vertical. DO NOT flare ribs ("donkey kick"). Head moves FORWARD then back (the "face escape").
- **Seated DB Press**: Bench at 85° (not 90° — too vertical). Start at shoulder height, palms forward. Press up and slightly together. Stop when arms are just short of lockout to keep tension.

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Leaning back (arching) | Brace core — weight is too heavy |
| Pressing in front of face | Keep bar over mid-foot / ears |
| Flaring elbows | Elbows slightly forward of bar |
| Not bracing | Breathe into 360° brace before each rep |
| Letting dumbbells drift apart | Control the arc — press together |

**Progressions**:
```
Beginner:   Machine Press → Dumbbell Press → Landmine Press
Intermediate: Standing OHP → Seated Military Press → Arnold Press
Advanced:   Push Press → Push Jerk → Behind-the-Neck Press (selective)
```

**Programming**: OHP responds best to moderate rep ranges (5-8). Heavy low-rep work (3-5) is effective but progress is slower than bench. Shoulder press 1-2x/week paired with horizontal pulls.

### 3.2 Raise Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Target | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Dumbbell Lateral Raise | Beginner | Light dumbbells | Medial delt | Slight forward lean |
| Cable Lateral Raise | Intermediate | Cable | Medial delt | Constant tension |
| Dumbbell Front Raise | Beginner | Dumbbells | Anterior delt | Palms down |
| Cable Front Raise | Intermediate | Cable | Anterior delt | Unilateral or bilateral |
| Bent-Over Dumbbell Lateral Raise | Intermediate | Dumbbells | Posterior delt | 45° hinge |
| Reverse Pec Deck Flye | Beginner | Machine | Posterior delt | Great for beginners |
| Face Pull | Beginner | Cable rope | Posterior delt, traps | External rotation emphasis |
| Single-Arm Cable Lateral Raise | Intermediate | Cable | Medial delt (stretch bias) | Body faces away from stack |
| Incline Lateral Raise | Advanced | Dumbbells | Medial delt (less trap involvement) | Lying face down on incline bench |
| Kneeling Lateral Raise | Intermediate | Dumbbells | Medial delt (reduces momentum) | Half-kneeling position |
| Lateral Raise with 1/4 Turn | Advanced | Dumbbells | Medial delt | Rotate thumbs down at top |
| Y-T-W Raises (Prone) | Intermediate | Light plates/no weight | Rear delt, rotator cuff | Rehab / prehab variation |

**Coaching Cues**:
- **Lateral Raise**: Dumbbells at thighs, slight forward lean. Lead with ELBOWS, not hands. Raise until arms parallel to floor. Slight bend in elbows (~15°). Thumbs slightly DOWN at top (internal rotation for medial delt focus).
- **Face Pull**: Rope at upper-chest height. Pull to face, separate the rope, squeeze rear delts. 2s hold. Do NOT lean back.
- **Bent-Over Raise**: Hinge at 45° (or chest-supported). Palms face each other. Raise to shoulder height. Squeeze rear delts.

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Using momentum to swing weight up | Drop weight — control the lift |
| Raising above parallel (traps take over) | Stop at shoulder height |
| Shrugging through the movement | Depress scapulae — keep shoulders down |
| Incorrect elbow positioning | Lead with elbows, not hands |
| Too heavy (compromised form) | Lateral raises want LIGHT weight, controlled |

**Programming**: Delts recover fast. Can train raises 3-4x/week, 2-4 sets of 10-20. Medial delt often needs the most volume for width. Isolation work after compound pressing, or on separate days.

## 4. Legs

### 4.1 Squat Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Primary Target | Secondary Target |
|-----------|------------|-----------|----------------|------------------|
| Goblet Squat | Beginner | Kettlebell / dumbbell | Quads, glutes | Core (holds weight in front) |
| Bodyweight Squat | Beginner | None | Quads, glutes | Core, balance |
| Barbell Back Squat (High-Bar) | Intermediate | Barbell + rack | Quads (upright torso) | Glutes, hamstrings |
| Barbell Back Squat (Low-Bar) | Advanced | Barbell + rack | Glutes, hamstrings | Posterior chain (lean forward) |
| Front Squat | Advanced | Barbell + rack | Quads (upright torso) | Core (anterior loading) |
| Safety Bar Squat | Intermediate | Safety bar | Quads, glutes | Shoulder-friendly alternative |
| Box Squat | Intermediate | Box/bench | Glutes, hamstrings | Powerlifting variation |
| Pause Squat | Advanced | Barbell + rack | Full leg (stretch reflex removal) | Core, control |
| Bulgarian Split Squat | Advanced | Dumbbells + bench | Quads (unilateral) | Glutes, balance, core |
| Hack Squat | Intermediate | Machine | Quads | Limited stabilizer work |
| Sissy Squat | Advanced | None / strap | Quadriceps (isolation) | Patellar stress risk |
| Sumo Squat / Plie Squat | Intermediate | Dumbbell or barbell | Adductors, quads | Wider stance |

**Coaching Cues**:
- **High-Bar Back Squat**: Bar rests on traps (NOT on cervical spine). Grip narrow. Elbows pointed DOWN. Break at hips AND knees simultaneously. Chest UP, elbows FORWARD to keep bar path over mid-foot. Depth: hip crease below knee.
- **Low-Bar Back Squat**: Bar rests on posterior delt shelf. Wrists neutral (not bent back). Torso leans forward more. Hips travel BACK more. Depth: hip crease below knee.
- **Front Squat**: Bar in clean rack position — fingers loose, two fingers or full front rack. Elbows HIGH. Upright torso — DO NOT let elbows drop (bar will dump).

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Knee valgus (caving in) | Push knees OUT — activate glutes |
| Good morning squat (hips rise faster than chest) | Stay braced — keep chest up |
| Lifting heels off floor | Work on ankle mobility OR use heeled shoes |
| Incomplete depth (high squats) | Work on mobility or drop weight |
| Bar on cervical spine (not traps) | Bar goes on traps/rear delts — NOT vertebra |
| Breathing incorrectly | Valsalva maneuver — big breath, brace, hold through rep |

**Progressions**:
```
Beginner:   Goblet Squat → Bodyweight Squat → High-Bar Back Squat (light)
Intermediate: High-Bar Back Squat → Front Squat → Bulgarian Split Squat
Advanced:   Low-Bar Back Squat (PL focus) → Pause Squat → Heavy Front Squat
```

**Programming**: Squat 1-3x/week. Heavier days (3-5 reps, 80-90%) and volume days (8-12 reps, 65-75%). Front squats complement back squats well on a second leg day. Unilateral work (Bulgarian split squat) as accessory.

### 4.2 Hinge / Deadlift Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Primary Target | Secondary Target |
|-----------|------------|-----------|----------------|------------------|
| Conventional Deadlift | Advanced | Barbell | Full posterior chain | Glutes, hamstrings, back |
| Sumo Deadlift | Advanced | Barbell | Glutes, adductors | Lower back (less demand) |
| Romanian Deadlift (RDL) | Intermediate | Barbell or dumbbells | Hamstrings, glutes | Erector spinae (isometric) |
| Straight-Leg Deadlift | Intermediate | Barbell | Hamstrings (deep stretch) | Lower back |
| Trap Bar Deadlift | Intermediate | Trap bar | Quads, legs | Safer for lower back |
| Single-Leg RDL | Intermediate | Dumbbell or kettlebell | Hamstrings (unilateral) | Balance, glutes |
| Rack Pull (Deadlift Partial) | Intermediate | Barbell + rack pins | Upper back, traps | Overload variation |
| Snatch-Grip Deadlift | Advanced | Barbell | Full posterior chain (greater ROM) | Traps, upper back |
| Deficit Deadlift | Advanced | Barbell + platform | Full ROM (stretch emphasis) | Quad drive off floor |
| Kettlebell Swing | Beginner | Kettlebell | Glutes, hamstrings (explosive) | Core, lower back |
| Good Morning | Advanced | Barbell | Hamstrings, glutes | Spinal erectors (high risk if poor form) |

**Coaching Cues**:
- **Conventional Deadlift**: Bar over mid-foot, ~1" from shins. Hinge at hips, grab bar, shins to bar. Chest up, lats ENGAGED ("bend the bar"). Push the floor away — do NOT pull with arms. Drag bar up the leg. Lockout: squeeze glutes, ribs down, hips through. Eccentric: hinge first, then bend knees.
- **RDL**: Slight bend in knees. Hinge at hips pushing glutes BACK. Bar slides down legs. Feel deep hamstring stretch. Return by driving hips forward. Maintain neutral spine throughout.

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Rounded lower back | Brace core — maintain neutral spine |
| Bar drifting away from body | Drag bar up your LEGS — shin contact |
| Jerking the bar off floor | Take slack out FIRST — then push |
| Over-extending at top (hyperextension) | Squeeze glutes — don't lean back |
| Bending knees too early on RDL | Knees stay soft — hips do the work |
| Belting too tight for bracing | Brace INTO the belt — expand 360° |

**Common Mistakes (cont.)**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Squatting the deadlift (hips too low) | Hips higher — hinge, don't squat |
| Looking in the mirror (craned neck) | Neutral spine — pick a spot 6-8 ft ahead |
| Mixed grip vs. hook grip confusion | Hook grip for OL; mixed or straps for DL |

**Progressions**:
```
Beginner:   Kettlebell Swing → Trap Bar DL → RDL
Intermediate: Conventional DL → Sumo DL → Single-Leg RDL
Advanced:   Rack Pull → Deficit DL → Snatch-Grip DL
```

**Programming**: Deadlift volume must be managed carefully — CNS demand is high. 1 heavy day/week (3-5 reps), 1 lighter speed day (speed pulls — 8x2 at 60%). RDLs as secondary hamstring work (3x8-12). Deadlift frequency: 1-2x/week max for most lifters.

## 5. Arms

### 5.1 Biceps Curl Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Target | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Standing Barbell Curl | Intermediate | Barbell | Long head + short head | Standard mass builder |
| Dumbbell Hammer Curl | Beginner | Dumbbells | Brachialis, brachioradialis | Neutral grip |
| Incline Dumbbell Curl | Intermediate | Dumbbells + incline bench | Long head (stretch bias) | Full stretch at bottom |
| Concentration Curl | Beginner | Dumbbell + bench | Short head | Strict form isolate |
| Standing Dumbbell Curl | Beginner | Dumbbells | Full biceps | Alternating or simultaneous |
| Preacher Curl | Intermediate | EZ bar + bench | Short head | Eliminates body swing |
| Cable Curl | Intermediate | Cable | Full biceps | Constant tension |
| Spider Curl | Advanced | Dumbbells + incline bench | Peak contraction | Face-down on incline |
| Bayesian Cable Curl | Intermediate | Cable (low pulley) | Full biceps | Unique angle, deep stretch |
| Zottman Curl | Intermediate | Dumbbells | Biceps + brachialis | Curl up, rotate down for eccentric |
| Reverse Curl | Intermediate | Barbell or EZ bar | Brachioradialis | Pronated grip |
| 21s (7-7-7) | Advanced | Barbell or dumbbells | Full biceps | Bottom 7, top 7, full 7 |

**Coaching Cues**:
- Keep wrists neutral — do not flex/extend at the top
- Elbows pinned to sides — do not drift forward
- Squeeze the pinky into the bar to bias short head; squeeze the thumb to bias long head
- Control the eccentric (2-3s) — the stretch is where growth happens
- DO NOT swing the weight — if you need momentum, it's too heavy

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Swinging / momentum on curls | Drop weight — pin elbows |
| Wrist flexion at top | Keep wrist straight — don't curl the hand |
| Not extending fully | Full elbow extension at bottom for stretch |
| Elbows flaring forward | Pin elbows to rib cage |
| Moving shoulders to help | Lock the shoulder joint — only forearm moves |

**Programming**: Biceps recover fast — train 2-3x/week, 6-12 sets per session. Higher reps (8-15) are effective. Pair with triceps in supersets (antagonistic). Brachialis work (hammer curls) builds overall arm thickness, especially viewed from the side.

### 5.2 Triceps Extension Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Target | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Cable Pushdown (straight bar) | Beginner | Cable + bar | Lateral + medial head | Best for overall mass |
| Cable Pushdown (rope) | Intermediate | Cable + rope | Lateral head | Full supination at bottom |
| Skull Crusher (Lying Triceps Extension) | Intermediate | EZ bar or dumbbells | Long head | Great mass builder |
| Close-Grip Bench Press | Intermediate | Barbell | All heads (Heavy) | Benefits from heavier load |
| Overhead Cable Extension | Intermediate | Cable | Long head (stretched) | Constant tension |
| Dumbbell Overhead Extension | Intermediate | Dumbbell | Long head | Single or both arms |
| Bench Dip | Beginner | Bench | All heads | Bodyweight |
| Parallel Bar Dip | Intermediate | Dip bars | All heads (chest if forward) | Can add weight |
| Triceps Kickback | Beginner | Dumbbells | Lateral head | Can use cable |
| French Press (EZ bar) | Intermediate | EZ bar | Long head | Seated or lying |
| Tate Press | Advanced | Dumbbells | All heads (inner bias) | Dumbbells from ears |

**Coaching Cues**:
- **Pushdown**: Elbows pinned to sides. Push down and slightly OUT at bottom. Squeeze at full extension for 1s. Control the return — DO NOT let the cable pull your arms up.
- **Skull Crusher**: Start with arms extended over shoulders. Lower bar to FOREHEAD (not behind). Elbows stay pointed toward ceiling — do NOT let them flare.
- **Overhead Extension**: Arm(s) extended overhead. Lower behind head. Deep triceps stretch. Return to start. Keep upper arms still — only forearm moves.

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Elbows flaring out on pushdowns | Pin elbows to sides |
| Bouncing off chest in dips | Control descent — no bounce |
| Lowering bar PAST head in skull crushers | Bar touches forehead, not beyond |
| Using too much shoulder | Only elbow moves — keep arm in line |
| Not extending fully on overhead work | Full elbow lockout at top |

**Progressions**:
```
Beginner:   Bench Dips → Cable Pushdown → Triceps Kickback
Intermediate: Skull Crushers → Close-Grip Bench → Parallel Bar Dips
Advanced:   Weighted Dips → Overhead Cable Extension → French Press (heavy)
```

**Programming**: Triceps make up ~2/3 of arm mass. Train 2-3x/week alongside chest and shoulder pressing. Pushdowns for day-to-day, overhead work for long head development. 9-15 sets/week for growth.

### 6.1 Anti-Extension (Anterior Core)

| Variation | Difficulty | Equipment | Target | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Front Plank | Beginner | Floor | Rectus abdominis, TVA | Foundation for all core work |
| Extended Plank (Arms forward) | Beginner | Floor | Rectus abdominis (harder lever) | Longer lever |
| Plank with Leg Lift | Intermediate | Floor | Rectus abdominis, hip flexors | Anti-rotation + hip flexor |
| Stability Ball Rollout | Advanced | Stability ball | Full anterior core | Great stretch + contraction |
| Ab Wheel Rollout | Advanced | Ab wheel | Full anterior core | Most intense rollout |
| TRX / Rings Fallout | Advanced | TRX or rings | Full anterior core | Instability challenge |

| Weighted Sit-Up | Intermediate | Weight plate | Rectus abdominis | Simple overload |
| Dragon Flag | Elite | Bench | Full core (extreme) | Bruce Lee classic |

**Coaching Cues**:
- **Plank**: Hips flat — DO NOT pike up. Push forearms into floor (external rotation cue). Squeeze glutes and quads. 360° brace. 3x30-60s for beginners.

**Progressions**:
```
Beginner:   Dead Bug → Plank (30s) → Plank (60s)
Intermediate: Weighted Plank → Decline Sit-Up → Ab Wheel (kneeling)
Advanced:   Ab Wheel (standing) → TRX Fallout → Dragon Flag
```

**Anti-Extension King**: Ab wheel rollout. Nothing builds anterior core tension faster.

**Rowing Technique** (The 4 Phases):

2. **Drive** — LEGS first (80%), then swing back, then PULL arms to lower ribs

**Common Mistakes**: Rowing with arms first (should be legs), rounding lower back at catch, over-sliding (shins past vertical = back strain), pulling handle too high (should hit lower ribs).

## 8. Olympic Lifts

### 8.1 Snatch

The snatch — bar from floor to overhead in one uninterrupted movement. The most technically demanding lift in strength sports.

| Variation | Difficulty | Purpose | Notes |
|-----------|------------|---------|-------|
| Full Snatch | Elite | Full lift | Competition version |
| Power Snatch | Advanced | Catch above parallel | Lighter loading, more vertical |
| Hang Snatch (from hip) | Advanced | Second pull focus | Eliminates first pull |
| Hang Snatch (from knee) | Advanced | Pull under focus | Longer pull phase |
| Snatch Pull | Advanced | Pull strength (no catch) | Overload — 110-120% of snatch |
| Snatch Deadlift | Intermediate | First pull strength | From floor to mid-thigh ONLY |
| Snatch High Pull | Intermediate | Second pull + extension | Triple extension focus |
| Muscle Snatch | Advanced | Overhead stability | No dip — strict press below |
| Snatch Balance | Advanced | Receiving position | OHS confidence, footwork |
| Snatch from Blocks | Elite | Specific positions | Reinforces good mechanics |
| Tempo Snatch | Advanced | Technique under fatigue | Slow eccentric, controlled |

**Snatch Progression Path**:
```
1. Overhead Squat (OHS) — absolute prerequisite
2. Snatch Grip Press + Snatch Grip Behind Neck Press
3. Snatch Deadlift (first pull)
4. Snatch High Pull (second pull)
5. Hang Power Snatch (catch above parallel)
6. Hang Snatch (full depth)
7. Full Snatch from floor
```

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Early arm bend | Arms are ROPES until bar passes hip |
| Jumping forward in catch | Pull BAR back — jump STRAIGHT UP |
| Not fully extended | Full hip + knee + ankle extension before pull under |
| Looping the bar out | Keep bar CLOSE — brush the shirt |
| Quarter-squat receiving | COMMIT to pulling UNDER — receive in full squat |
| Cat-back rounding in set-up | Flat back — proud chest — engaged lats |

**Key Coaching Cues**:
- "Push the floor away" — the bar rises because you push with your legs
- "Tall, then small" — extend fully, then drop UNDER the bar
- "Elbows HIGH and BACK" in the pull under
- "Stomp / punch the ground" when transitioning from pull to catch
- Bar path should be straight up — closer to the body than most beginners think

### 8.2 Clean & Jerk

Clean: bar from floor to shoulders. Jerk: bar from shoulders to overhead.

#### Clean Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Purpose | Notes |
|-----------|------------|---------|-------|
| Full Clean (Squat Clean) | Elite | Full lift | Competition |
| Power Clean | Advanced | Catch above parallel | Lower volume, more explosive |
| Hang Clean (from hip) | Advanced | Second pull focus | Eliminates first pull |
| Hang Clean (from knee) | Advanced | Pull-under timing | Longer pull phase |
| Clean Pull | Advanced | Pull strength | 110-120% of clean |
| Clean Deadlift | Intermediate | First pull position | Floor to mid-thigh |
| Clean High Pull | Intermediate | Second pull + extension | Triple extension emphasis |
| Muscle Clean | Intermediate | Front rack position | No dip — strict pull and catch |
| Clean from Blocks | Elite | Specific positions | Reinforces position work |
| Complex: Clean + Front Squat | Advanced | Squat strength | Clean + 1-3 front squats |
| Tempo Clean | Advanced | Technique under pressure | Slow descent, controlled |

#### Jerk Variations

| Variation | Difficulty | Purpose | Notes |
|-----------|------------|---------|-------|
| Push Press | Intermediate | Leg drive, dip timing | Primer for jerk |
| Push Jerk | Advanced | Partial dip + re-dip | Faster than split jerk |
| Split Jerk | Elite | Full lift | Competition standard |
| Power Jerk | Elite | No split, dip only | Some prefer over split |
| Jerk Balance | Advanced | Receiving position | Confidence in split |
| Press in Split | Intermediate | Overhead strength | Isolate jerk lockout |
| Split Press | Intermediate | Split stability | Weight in split position |

**Clean Progression Path**:
```
1. Front Squat — prerequisite
2. Clean Deadlift (first pull)
3. Clean High Pull (second pull)
4. Muscle Clean (no dip)
5. Hang Power Clean (catch above parallel)
6. Hang Clean (full depth)
7. Full Clean from floor
```

**Jerk Progression Path**:
```
1. Press (OH strength)
2. Push Press (dip + drive)
3. Push Jerk (dip + drive + re-dip)
4. Split Jerk (with empty bar — FOOTWORK)
5. Split Jerk (light — build to full)
```

**Common Mistakes**:
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Contacting bar too high (hip) | Brush mid-thigh — contact point for clean is different from snatch |
| Bar crashing on front rack | Meet the bar with elbows UP — don't let it crash |
| Pressing out the jerk | LOCK OUT at full extension — no press out |
| Split too narrow / too wide | Front knee at 90°, back leg straight, both feet flat |
| Front foot landing early | DIP down — DRIVE up — SPLIT under — simultaneous |
| Losing bar forward in front squat | Elbows UP — tight upper back |

**Key Cues**:
- Clean: "Bar up the LEGS, not the chest"
- Clean: "Triple extension — hips, knees, ankles" at the top of the pull
- Clean: "Elbows FAST" in the turnover
- Jerk: "Dip STRAIGHT" — don't push hips forward
- Jerk: "Front knee out, back knee straight" in the split
- Jerk: "Stand the bar up" — stand up from split with bar overhead before putting down

**Programming**: OL lifting PRIORITIZES technique over load. 80-90% of training should be at 70-85% 1RM. Full lifts 1-3 reps per set. Pulls and squats for strength. Snatch + Clean & Jerk 4-5x/week (alternating emphasis). Squat every session.

**Sample Beginner Session**:
```
1. Snatch Technique (empty bar + light): 10-15 sets of 2-3
2. Clean & Jerk Technique: 10-12 sets of 2-3
3. Front Squat: 3x5
4. Pulls (Snatch or Clean): 3x5 @ 80-90% of best pull
5. Core / Accessory: 10 min
```

**Safety & Programming Rules**:
- Plyos are GROUND REACTION training — quality over quantity
- Stop when landing quality degrades (heavy breathing = poor landings)
- Beginners: 40-80 total ground contacts per session
- Advanced: 80-120 total ground contacts per session
- Rest: 60-90s between sets (full recovery for quality)
- Frequency: 1-2x/week for beginners, 2-3x/week for advanced
- Never train plyometrics on consecutive days

**Landing Mechanics**:
- "Soft landings" — land from toe to heel, absorb through hips/knees
- Knees TRACK over toes — NO valgus collapse
- Hips hinge back on impact — do NOT land stiff-legged
- "Land like a ninja" — quiet landings indicate good absorption
- Stick the landing for 2s before next rep on box jumps

**Phase Progression**:
```
Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Low intensity (pogos, low box jumps) — technique focus
Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8): Moderate intensity (squat jumps, broad jumps, lateral)
Phase 3 (Weeks 9-12): High intensity (depth jumps, bounding)
Phase 4 (Weeks 13-16): Sport-specific / maximal
```

## Safety Considerations — All Categories

| Factor | Guideline |
|--------|-----------|
| **Warm-Up** | 5-10 min dynamic warm-up + 2-5 ramp-up sets before working sets |
| **Progressive Overload** | 2.5-5% load increase per week (upper), 5-10% (lower) |
| **Deload** | Every 4-6 weeks — reduce volume 40-60% or intensity 10-20% |
| **Pain vs. Discomfort** | Muscle burn = fine; joint pain = STOP |
| **Spotting** | Always spot heavy bench (3+ reps in reserve rule) |
| **Rest Periods** | Strength: 3-5min; Hypertrophy: 60-90s; Endurance: 30-45s |
| **Overtraining Signs** | Persistent fatigue, sleep disruption, declining performance, mood changes |
| **Form First** | Every rep count — bad reps train bad patterns |
| **Bracing** | 360° breath — brace in ALL directions (front, back, sides) |
| **Range of Motion** | Full ROM unless intentionally partial for overload. Partial = tool, not default |
| **Equipment Check** | Collars on all barbells. Inspect cables for fraying. Clip plates securely |
| **Footwear** | Flat shoes (Converse, deadlift slippers) or barefoot for squats/deadlifts; running shoes for cardio |
| **Neck Protection** | Bar NEVER rests on cervical spine in squats. Hands guide but do not pull in situps |

### Training Splits (Common Approaches)

| Split | Layout | Best For |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Full Body | 3x/week | Beginners, limited days |
| Upper/Lower | 4x/week | General strength, intermediates |
| Push/Pull/Legs | 3-6x/week | Hypertrophy, intermediates |
| Body Part (Bro Split) | 5-6x/week | Advanced bodybuilding |
| Block Periodization | Variable | Peak performance, sport-specific |

### Variation Selection Rules of Thumb

- **Main lifts**: 1-2 compound movements per session (bench, squat, deadlift, OHP, pull-ups)
- **Accessories**: 2-4 movements per session covering weaknesses
- **Exercise rotation**: Change primary variation every 4-8 weeks
- **Sticking point**: Target the weak ROM with specific variations (board press for lockout, pause squat for bottom)
- **Maintenance vs. Focus**: 1-2 focus lifts get the most volume; others are maintenance
