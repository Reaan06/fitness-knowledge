# Nutrition & Macros — Complete Guide

## 1. Caloric Fundamentals

### Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)
- Calories burned at rest
- Calculated via Mifflin-St Jeor or Harris-Benedict equations
- Varies by age, weight, height, gender, muscle mass

### Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)
- BMR + Activity Level + Thermic Effect of Food
- Activity multipliers:
  - Sedentary: BMR × 1.2
  - Light activity: BMR × 1.375
  - Moderate activity: BMR × 1.55
  - Very active: BMR × 1.725
  - Extra active: BMR × 1.9

### Caloric Balance
- **Maintenance**: TDEE = Calories consumed
- **Fat loss**: TDEE - 300-500 calories (deficit)
- **Muscle gain**: TDEE + 200-400 calories (surplus)
- **Recomp**: TDEE ± 100 calories with high protein

## 2. Macronutrients

### Protein (Proteínas)
- **Function**: Muscle repair, growth, immune function
- **Calories**: 4 cal/gram
- **Recommended**:
  - Sedentary: 0.8g/kg body weight
  - Active: 1.2-1.6g/kg
  - Strength athletes: 1.6-2.2g/kg
  - Deficit phases: 2.0-2.4g/kg (preserves muscle)
- **Best sources**: Chicken, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, tofu
- **Timing**: 20-40g per meal, spread throughout day

### Carbohydrates (Carbohidratos)
- **Function**: Primary energy source, brain fuel, muscle glycogen
- **Calories**: 4 cal/gram
- **Types**:
  - **Simple**: Fast absorption (sugar, fruits, white rice)
  - **Complex**: Slow absorption (oats, sweet potatoes, whole grains)
  - **Fiber**: Indigestible (vegetables, legumes, whole grains)
- **Recommended**:
  - Sedentary: 2-3g/kg
  - Moderate activity: 3-5g/kg
  - High intensity: 5-8g/kg
  - Endurance athletes: 6-10g/kg
- **Timing**:
  - Pre-workout: 1-2 hours before
  - Intra-workout: For sessions >60 minutes
  - Post-workout: Within 2 hours

### Fats (Grasas)
- **Function**: Hormone production, vitamin absorption, cell structure
- **Calories**: 9 cal/gram
- **Types**:
  - **Unsaturated** (good): Olive oil, avocado, nuts, fatty fish
  - **Saturated** (moderate): Red meat, butter, coconut oil
  - **Trans** (avoid): Processed foods, fried foods
- **Recommended**: 20-35% of total calories
- **Essential**: Omega-3 (fish, flaxseed) and Omega-6 (nuts, seeds)

## 3. Micronutrients

### Vitamins
- **Fat-soluble**: A, D, E, K (store in fat tissue)
- **Water-soluble**: B-complex, C (excess excreted)
- **Key for fitness**:
  - B12: Energy, red blood cells
  - D: Bone health, immune function
  - C: Recovery, immune function
  - E: Antioxidant, cell protection

### Minerals
- **Macrominerals**: Calcium, potassium, sodium, magnesium
- **Trace minerals**: Iron, zinc, selenium, chromium
- **Key for fitness**:
  - Iron: Oxygen transport (critical for endurance)
  - Zinc: Testosterone, recovery
  - Magnesium: Muscle function, sleep
  - Calcium: Bone health, muscle contraction

## 4. Hydration

### Water Needs
- **Baseline**: 35-40ml per kg body weight
- **Exercise**: +500-1000ml per hour of exercise
- **Hot climate**: +500ml additional
- **Signs of dehydration**: Dark urine, headache, fatigue

### Electrolytes
- **Sodium**: Lost in sweat, essential for nerve function
- **Potassium**: Muscle contractions, fluid balance
- **Magnesium**: Muscle relaxation, sleep quality

### Timing
- **Pre-exercise**: 500ml 2-3 hours before
- **During**: 150-250ml every 15-20 minutes
- **Post-exercise**: 1.5L per kg lost during exercise

## 5. Meal Timing & Frequency

### Meal Frequency
- **3-6 meals per day**: Spread protein intake
- **No magic number**: Consistency matters more
- **Pre-sleep**: Casein protein (slow absorption)

### Nutrient Timing
- **Pre-workout** (1-2 hours): Carbs + moderate protein
- **Post-workout** (within 2 hours): Protein + carbs
- **Before bed**: Protein + healthy fats

## 6. Special Diets

### Mediterranean Diet
- High: Olive oil, fish, vegetables, whole grains
- Moderate: Wine, dairy
- Low: Red meat, processed foods
- Benefits: Heart health, longevity

### Ketogenic Diet
- Very low carb (<50g/day)
- High fat (70-80% calories)
- Moderate protein
- Benefits: Fat loss, mental clarity
- Drawbacks: Performance, sustainability

### Intermittent Fasting
- 16:8, 18:6, or 20:4 protocols
- Benefits: Fat loss, insulin sensitivity
- Drawbacks: May affect performance

### Plant-Based
- No animal products
- Requires: B12 supplementation, iron monitoring
- Benefits: Health, environment
- Challenges: Protein completeness

## 7. Body Composition

### Measurements
- **Body fat percentage**: Calipers, DEXA, bioimpedance
- **Waist-to-hip ratio**: Health indicator
- **BMI**: Limitations (doesn't distinguish muscle/fat)

### Healthy Ranges
- **Men**: 10-20% body fat
- **Women**: 18-28% body fat
- **Athletes**: Lower ranges

### Recomposition
- Building muscle while losing fat
- Requires: High protein, slight deficit, strength training
- Best for: Beginners, returning to training
