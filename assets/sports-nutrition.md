# Advanced Sports Nutrition

## 1. Nutrient Timing Strategies

### Pre-Workout Nutrition (2-3 hours before)
- **Carbs**: 1-2g/kg for endurance, 0.5-1g/kg for strength
- **Protein**: 20-30g (whey or lean meat)
- **Fat**: Minimal (slows digestion)
- **Examples**: Oatmeal with protein, rice with chicken, banana with yogurt

### Intra-Workout Nutrition
- **For sessions <60 minutes**: Water only
- **For sessions 60-90 minutes**: 30-60g carbs/hour (sports drink)
- **For sessions >90 minutes**: 60-90g carbs/hour (mix of glucose + fructose)
- **Electrolytes**: Essential for heavy sweaters

### Post-Workout Nutrition (within 2 hours)
- **Protein**: 0.3-0.5g/kg (20-40g for most people)
- **Carbs**: 0.8-1.2g/kg (higher for endurance athletes)
- **Timing**: Earlier is better, but total daily intake matters more
- **Examples**: Protein shake with banana, chicken with rice, Greek yogurt with fruit

## 2. Performance Nutrition

### Carbohydrate Loading
- **Purpose**: Maximize glycogen stores before competition
- **Protocol**: 3-5 days of high carbs (8-10g/kg/day)
- **Best for**: Endurance events >90 minutes
- **Avoid**: Fiber, fat, new foods

### Fat Loading
- **Purpose**: Enhance fat oxidation for ultra-endurance
- **Protocol**: High fat (60-70% calories) for 2-3 weeks
- **Drawbacks**: May impair high-intensity performance
- **Best for**: Ultra-marathons, ironman

### Caffeine
- **Dose**: 3-6mg/kg body weight
- **Timing**: 30-60 minutes before exercise
- **Benefits**: Alertness, endurance, power, pain reduction
- **Tolerance**: Cycle use to maintain effectiveness
- **Caution**: Can cause jitters, insomnia, increased heart rate

### Beta-Alanine
- **Dose**: 3.2-6.4g/day (split doses)
- **Timing**: With meals
- **Benefits**: Buffers lactic acid, improves endurance
- **Side effect**: Paresthesia (tingling) - harmless

### Creatine
- **Dose**: 3-5g/day (maintenance)
- **Loading**: Optional 20g/day for 5-7 days
- **Benefits**: Strength, power, muscle growth, recovery
- **Safety**: Well-researched, safe for healthy individuals
- **Timing**: Anytime, consistency matters

## 3. Recovery Nutrition

### Anti-Inflammatory Foods
- **Omega-3**: Fatty fish, flaxseed, walnuts
- **Berries**: Blueberries, strawberries, cherries
- **Leafy greens**: Spinach, kale, broccoli
- **Turmeric**: Curcumin (absorb with black pepper)
- **Ginger**: Reduces muscle soreness

### Muscle Recovery
- **Protein**: 20-40g within 2 hours post-exercise
- **Carbs**: Replenish glycogen stores
- **Tart cherry juice**: Reduces muscle soreness
- **Sleep**: Primary recovery mechanism

### Sleep Optimization for Recovery
- **Duration**: 7-9 hours per night
- **Consistency**: Same sleep/wake time
- **Environment**: Cool, dark, quiet
- **Pre-sleep**: Avoid screens, caffeine, heavy meals

## 4. Weight Management Nutrition

### Fat Loss Phase
- **Deficit**: 300-500 calories below TDEE
- **Protein**: Increase to 2.0-2.4g/kg (preserves muscle)
- **Carbs**: Moderate (2-3g/kg)
- **Fats**: Minimum 0.5g/kg (hormone health)
- **Duration**: 8-16 weeks maximum
- **Refeed**: 1-2 days at maintenance every 2-3 weeks

### Muscle Gain Phase (Bulking)
- **Surplus**: 200-400 calories above TDEE
- **Protein**: 1.6-2.2g/kg
- **Carbs**: High (4-6g/kg)
- **Fats**: 0.8-1.2g/kg
- **Duration**: 12-24 weeks
- **Goal**: Minimize fat gain

### Maintenance Phase
- **Calories**: At TDEE
- **Purpose**: Diet breaks, mental recovery
- **Duration**: 2-4 weeks between phases

## 5. Special Considerations

### Vegetarian/Vegan Athletes
- **Protein combining**: Legumes + grains
- **Supplements**: B12, iron, omega-3 (algae-based)
- **Key sources**: Tofu, tempeh, lentils, quinoa, nuts

### Older Athletes (>50 years)
- **Protein**: Higher needs (1.2-1.6g/kg)
- **Calcium**: Bone health
- **Vitamin D**: Often deficient
- **Collagen**: Joint health

### Female Athletes
- **Iron**: Higher needs due to menstruation
- **Calcium**: Bone health
- **Caloric needs**: Avoid Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport (RED-S)
- **Cycle considerations**: May affect performance

### Hot/Cold Climate
- **Hot**: Increase fluids, electrolytes, carbs
- **Cold**: Increase calories, warm fluids, carbs

## 6. Supplements Evidence-Based Guide

### Tier 1: Strong Evidence
- **Creatine monohydrate**: 3-5g/day
- **Caffeine**: 3-6mg/kg
- **Beta-alanine**: 3.2-6.4g/day
- **Protein powder**: Convenience for meeting protein needs

### Tier 2: Moderate Evidence
- **Omega-3 fish oil**: 2-3g EPA+DHA/day
- **Vitamin D**: 1000-2000 IU/day (if deficient)
- **Magnesium**: 200-400mg/day
- **Zinc**: 15-30mg/day

### Tier 3: Limited Evidence
- **BCAAs**: Unnecessary if protein intake is adequate
- **Glutamine**: Minimal benefit for healthy individuals
- **HMB**: Limited benefits for trained athletes

### Avoid
- **Fat burners**: Often unsafe, ineffective
- **Testosterone boosters**: Ineffective
- **Detox teas**: No scientific basis
- **Most "proprietary blends"**: Under-dosed, untested
