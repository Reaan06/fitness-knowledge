# Personalized Training Programs Module

## User Assessment Framework

### Step 1: Gather User Information

**Essential Data:**
- Age
- Gender
- Current weight (kg/lb)
- Height (cm/ft)
- Body fat % (if known)
- Training experience (Beginner/Intermediate/Advanced)
- Available equipment (Home/Gym/Minimal)
- Training days per week
- Time per session
- Primary goal (Lose Weight/Gain Muscle/Improve Performance/General Health)
- Any injuries or limitations

**Optional Data:**
- Current fitness level (can they do basic exercises?)
- Dietary restrictions
- Sleep quality
- Stress level
- Occupation (sedentary/active)

### Step 2: Calculate Requirements

Using calculators module:
1. Calculate BMR
2. Calculate TDEE
3. Determine caloric goal based on objective
4. Calculate macros based on goal

### Step 3: Design Program

#### Training Age Classification

| Level | Experience | Characteristics |
|-------|------------|-----------------|
| Beginner | 0-6 months | Learning form, building base |
| Intermediate | 6-24 months | Progressive overload, specialization |
| Advanced | 24+ months | Periodization, specificity |

#### Frequency Guidelines

| Goal | Minimum Frequency | Optimal Frequency |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Weight Loss | 3 days/week | 4-5 days/week |
| Muscle Gain | 3 days/week | 4-6 days/week |
| Strength | 2 days/week | 3-4 days/week |
| Endurance | 3 days/week | 5-6 days/week |

#### Time Per Session

| Duration | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| 30 min | Full body, efficiency |
| 45-60 min | Standard training |
| 60-90 min | Advanced, sport-specific |
| 90+ min | Elite athletes only |

## Program Templates

### Beginner Full Body (3 Days/Week)

**Day 1: Full Body A**
1. Barbell Squat: 3×10
2. Bench Press: 3×10
3. Bent-Over Row: 3×10

3. Dumbbell Lunges: 3×10 each leg
4. Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3×10
5. Russian Twists: 3×12 each side

**Day 3: Full Body C**
1. Leg Press: 3×12
2. Incline Dumbbell Press: 3×10
3. Lat Pulldown: 3×10
4. Lateral Raises: 3×12
5. Face Pulls: 3×15

**Day 3: Legs**
1. Barbell Squat: 4×8

**Day 4: Upper Body**
1. Overhead Press: 4×8
2. Bench Press: 3×10
3. Pull-Ups: 3×8
4. Dumbbell Rows: 3×10

**Day 5: Lower Body**

### Advanced Periodized (5-6 Days/Week)

**Week 1-4: Hypertrophy Phase**
- Higher volume (12-15 reps)
- Moderate weight (65-75% 1RM)
- Shorter rest (60-90 sec)

**Week 5-8: Strength Phase**
- Lower volume (6-8 reps)
- Heavier weight (80-90% 1RM)
- Longer rest (2-3 min)

**Week 9-12: Power Phase**
- Very low volume (1-5 reps)
- Near max weight (90-100% 1RM)
- Maximum rest (3-5 min)

**Week 13: Deload**
- Light volume
- Light weight (50-60% 1RM)
- Focus on recovery

## Goal-Specific Programs

### Weight Loss Program

**Training Focus:**
- Resistance training to preserve muscle
- Moderate cardio for calorie burn
- High intensity for metabolic boost

**Sample Structure:**
- 3-4 resistance training days
- 2-3 cardio sessions (mix of LISS and HIIT)
- Active recovery days

**Key Principles:**
- Progressive overload on main lifts
- Keep intensity high (70-85% 1RM)
- Include metabolic conditioning
- Track calories and macros strictly

### Muscle Gain Program

**Training Focus:**
- Progressive overload
- Adequate volume (10-20 sets per muscle group/week)
- Compound movements as foundation

**Sample Structure:**
- 4-6 training days
- Split routines (PPL, Upper/Lower, Body Part)
- 48-72 hours between training same muscle

**Key Principles:**
- Progressive overload (weight, reps, or sets)
- Sufficient protein (1.6-2.2 g/kg)
- Caloric surplus (300-500 calories)
- Adequate sleep (7-9 hours)

### Strength Program

**Training Focus:**
- Heavy compound lifts
- Low reps, high weight
- Long rest periods

**Sample Structure:**
- 3-4 training days
- Focus on squat, bench, deadlift
- Accessory work for weak points

**Key Principles:**
- Progressive overload (weight focus)
- Intensity over volume
- Long rest (3-5 min between sets)
- Periodization (linear or undulating)

### General Health Program

**Training Focus:**
- Balanced approach
- Variety of modalities
- Enjoyment and sustainability

**Sample Structure:**
- 3-5 training days
- Mix of strength, cardio, flexibility
- Functional movements

**Key Principles:**
- Consistency over intensity
- Find activities you enjoy
- Balance all fitness components
- Prioritize recovery

## AI Personalization Process

### Conversation Flow

1. **Assessment Phase**
   - Ask about goals, experience, equipment
   - Gather physical data (age, weight, height)
   - Identify limitations or injuries
   - Determine available time

2. **Calculation Phase**
   - Calculate BMR, TDEE
   - Determine caloric and macro needs
   - Estimate training capacity

3. **Program Design Phase**
   - Select appropriate template
   - Adjust for individual needs
   - Set progression plan
   - Establish check-in schedule

4. **Implementation Phase**
   - Explain program clearly
   - Demonstrate exercises
   - Set tracking system
   - Plan progress reviews

### Adaptive Programming

**When to Modify Program:**
- User consistently misses workouts
- Progress stalls for 2+ weeks
- User reports excessive fatigue
- Injury or limitation arises
- Goal changes

**Modification Options:**
- Adjust volume (sets × reps)
- Adjust intensity (% 1RM)
- Adjust frequency (days/week)
- Change exercise selection
- Modify rest periods

### Progress Tracking

**Metrics to Monitor:**
- Body weight (weekly average)
- Body measurements (monthly)
- Strength progress (weight/reps)
- Progress photos (monthly)
- Energy levels (daily)
- Sleep quality (daily)
- Mood/motivation (daily)

**Review Schedule:**
- Weekly: Check adherence, adjust as needed
- Monthly: Review progress, modify program
- Quarterly: Reassess goals, major program change

## Safety Considerations

### Red Flags to Watch For
- Sharp pain during exercise
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Chest pain or pressure
- Extreme fatigue lasting days
- Loss of motivation lasting weeks
- Obsessive behaviors around food/exercise

### When to Refer to Professional
- Medical conditions (heart disease, diabetes, etc.)
- Eating disorders or disordered eating
- Chronic pain or injuries
- Mental health concerns
- Advanced athletes seeking performance optimization

### Injury Modification Guidelines

| Injury Type | Avoid | Modify To |
|-------------|-------|-----------|
| Knee Pain | Deep squats, lunges | Leg press, step-ups |
| Back Pain | Deadlifts, bent-over rows | Machine rows, cable exercises |
| Shoulder Pain | Overhead press, bench press | Floor press, landmine press |
| Wrist Pain | Front squats, clean & jerk | goblet squat, dumbbell exercises |

## Integration Notes

### How AI Should Use This Module
1. Gather user information through conversation
2. Calculate personalized requirements
3. Select and adapt appropriate program
4. Explain program clearly with reasoning
5. Set up tracking and check-in system
6. Monitor progress and adjust as needed

### Communication Tips
- Use analogies users understand
- Break complex concepts into simple steps
- Provide context for recommendations
- Encourage questions and feedback
- Celebrate small wins

### Common User Concerns
- "I don't have time" → Efficient workout options
- "I can't afford gym" → Home/bodyweight alternatives
- "I'm too old/out of shape" → Beginner modifications
- "I've tried before and failed" → Address barriers, start small
