# Supplements — Evidence-Based Guide

## 1. Tier 1: Strong Evidence

### Creatine Monohydrate
- **Benefits**: Strength, power, muscle growth, recovery
- **Dose**: 3-5g/day (maintenance)
- **Loading**: Optional 20g/day for 5-7 days
- **Timing**: Anytime, consistency matters
- **Safety**: Well-researched, safe for healthy individuals
- **Forms**: Monohydrate is most researched, cheapest

### Caffeine
- **Benefits**: Alertness, endurance, power, pain reduction
- **Dose**: 3-6mg/kg body weight
- **Timing**: 30-60 minutes before exercise
- **Sources**: Coffee, tea, pre-workout supplements
- **Tolerance**: Cycle use to maintain effectiveness
- **Caution**: Can cause jitters, insomnia, increased heart rate

### Beta-Alanine
- **Benefits**: Buffers lactic acid, improves endurance
- **Dose**: 3.2-6.4g/day (split doses)
- **Timing**: With meals
- **Side effect**: Paresthesia (tingling) - harmless
- **Best for**: Endurance, high-rep training

### Protein Powder
- **Benefits**: Convenient protein source
- **Types**: Whey, casein, plant-based (pea, rice, soy)
- **Dose**: 20-40g per serving
- **Timing**: Post-workout, between meals
- **Considerations**: Total daily protein matters more than timing

## 2. Tier 2: Moderate Evidence

### Omega-3 Fish Oil
- **Benefits**: Anti-inflammatory, heart health, brain function
- **Dose**: 2-3g EPA+DHA per day
- **Timing**: With meals
- **Sources**: Fatty fish, fish oil supplements
- **Considerations**: Quality matters, look for third-party tested

### Vitamin D
- **Benefits**: Bone health, immune function, mood
- **Dose**: 1000-2000 IU/day (if deficient)
- **Timing**: With fat-containing meal
- **Testing**: Get blood levels checked
- **Considerations**: Many people are deficient, especially in winter

### Magnesium
- **Benefits**: Muscle relaxation, sleep, recovery
- **Dose**: 200-400mg/day
- **Timing**: Before bed for sleep benefits
- **Forms**: Glycinate (sleep), citrate (general), threonate (brain)
- **Considerations**: Most diets are deficient

### Zinc
- **Benefits**: Testosterone, recovery, immune function
- **Dose**: 15-30mg/day
- **Timing**: With food
- **Considerations**: Don't exceed 40mg/day long-term

### Ashwagandha
- **Benefits**: Stress reduction, recovery, testosterone
- **Dose**: 300-600mg/day
- **Timing**: Morning or evening
- **Forms**: KSM-66, Sensoril
- **Considerations**: May interact with medications

## 3. Tier 3: Limited Evidence

### BCAAs (Branched-Chain Amino Acids)
- **Benefits**: May reduce muscle soreness
- **Reality**: Unnecessary if protein intake is adequate
- **Dose**: 5-10g (if used)
- **Timing**: During workout
- **Considerations**: Waste of money for most people

### Glutamine
- **Benefits**: May support immune function
- **Reality**: Minimal benefit for healthy individuals
- **Dose**: 5-10g (if used)
- **Timing**: Post-workout
- **Considerations**: Body produces enough naturally

### HMB (Beta-Hydroxy Beta-Methylbutyrate)
- **Benefits**: May reduce muscle breakdown
- **Reality**: Limited benefits for trained athletes
- **Dose**: 3g/day
- **Timing**: Split doses with meals
- **Considerations**: More effective for beginners

### Citrulline Malate
- **Benefits**: May improve endurance, reduce soreness
- **Dose**: 6-8g pre-workout
- **Timing**: 30-60 minutes before exercise
- **Considerations**: Evidence is mixed

### Nitrate (Beetroot Juice)
- **Benefits**: May improve endurance
- **Dose**: 6-8mmol nitrate
- **Timing**: 2-3 hours before exercise
- **Considerations**: More effective for endurance than strength

## 4. Avoid (Ineffective or Unsafe)

### Fat Burners
- **Reality**: Often unsafe, ineffective
- **Risks**: Heart palpitations, anxiety, liver damage
- **Better approach**: Caloric deficit, exercise

### Testosterone Boosters
- **Reality**: Ineffective for most men
- **Risks**: May contain unlisted ingredients
- **Better approach**: Sleep, diet, exercise

### Detox Teas
- **Reality**: No scientific basis
- **Risks**: Laxative effects, dehydration
- **Better approach**: Liver and kidneys handle detox

### Proprietary Blends
- **Reality**: Under-dosed, untested
- **Risks**: Unknown ingredients, contamination
- **Better approach**: Individual ingredients, transparent labels

### DHEA
- **Reality**: May cause hormonal imbalance
- **Risks**: Acne, hair loss, mood changes
- **Better approach**: Natural testosterone optimization

## 5. Supplement Timing Guide

### Pre-Workout (30-60 minutes before)
- Caffeine: 3-6mg/kg
- Citrulline Malate: 6-8g
- Beta-Alanine: 3.2g
- Creatine: 3-5g (anytime)

### Intra-Workout (during exercise)
- BCAAs: 5-10g (if protein is low)
- Electrolytes: If sweating heavily
- Carbs: For sessions >60 minutes

### Post-Workout (within 2 hours)
- Protein: 20-40g
- Creatine: 3-5g (anytime)
- Carbs: 0.8-1.2g/kg

### Before Bed
- Magnesium: 200-400mg
- Casein protein: 20-40g (slow absorption)
- ZMA: If deficient in zinc/magnesium

## 6. Quality & Safety

### Third-Party Testing
- **Look for**: NSF, Informed Sport, USP verification
- **Avoid**: Unverified brands, proprietary blends
- **Check**: Batch testing, contamination screening

### Storage
- **Keep**: Cool, dry place
- **Avoid**: Heat, moisture, direct sunlight
- **Check**: Expiration dates

### Interactions
- **Medications**: Always consult healthcare provider
- **Health conditions**: Some supplements contraindicated
- **Pregnancy**: Many supplements unsafe

## 7. Budget-Friendly Options

### Essential (Must-Have)
- Creatine monohydrate: ~$15/month
- Protein powder: ~$30/month
- Caffeine (coffee): ~$10/month

### Optional (Nice-to-Have)
- Omega-3 fish oil: ~$15/month
- Magnesium: ~$10/month
- Vitamin D: ~$5/month

### Skip (Save Your Money)
- BCAAs
- Glutamine
- Testosterone boosters
- Fat burners
- Most pre-workouts (make your own)

## 8. Individual Considerations

### Vegetarians/Vegans
- **B12**: Essential supplement
- **Iron**: May need more
- **Omega-3**: Algae-based EPA/DHA
- **Protein**: Plant-based blends

### Older Adults (>50 years)
- **Vitamin D**: Often deficient
- **Calcium**: Bone health
- **Protein**: Higher needs
- **Collagen**: Joint health

### Women
- **Iron**: Higher needs due to menstruation
- **Calcium**: Bone health
- **Folate**: If planning pregnancy
- **Vitamin D**: Often deficient

### Athletes
- **Creatine**: Strong evidence
- **Caffeine**: Performance boost
- **Beta-alanine**: Endurance
- **Electrolytes**: Replacement during exercise
