# Common Mistakes — How to Fix Them

## 1. Training Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Ego Lifting
- **Problem**: Using too much weight, poor form
- **Consequences**: Injuries, no progress, bad habits
- **Solution**: Lower weight, focus on form, progressive overload
- **Rule**: If form breaks, weight is too heavy

### Mistake 2: Skipping Warm-up
- **Problem**: Cold muscles, increased injury risk
- **Consequences**: Strains, pulls, reduced performance
- **Solution**: 5-10 minutes dynamic warm-up
- **Protocol**: Light cardio + dynamic stretches + activation

### Mistake 3: Poor Form
- **Problem**: Wrong movement patterns
- **Consequences**: Injuries, muscle imbalances, no progress
- **Solution**: Learn proper technique, film yourself, hire coach
- **Rule**: Quality over quantity

### Mistake 4: No Progressive Overload
- **Problem**: Same weight/reps every session
- **Consequences**: Plateaus, no adaptation
- **Solution**: Increase weight, reps, or sets gradually
- **Rule**: Aim for small improvements weekly

### Mistake 5: Too Much Volume
- **Problem**: Overtraining, no recovery
- **Consequences**: Fatigue, decreased performance, injuries
- **Solution**: Listen to body, program deloads
- **Rule**: More is not always better

### Mistake 6: Ignoring Weak Points
- **Problem**: Only training strengths
- **Consequences**: Imbalances, injuries
- **Solution**: Prioritize weak areas
- **Rule**: Train what you suck at

### Mistake 7: No Rest Days
- **Problem**: No recovery time
- **Consequences**: Overtraining, burnout
- **Solution**: 1-2 rest days per week minimum
- **Rule**: Rest is when you grow

### Mistake 8: Inconsistent Training
- **Problem**: Sporadic workouts
- **Consequences**: No progress, wasted effort
- **Solution**: Schedule workouts, treat like appointments
- **Rule**: Consistency beats intensity

## 2. Nutrition Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Not Eating Enough Protein
- **Problem**: Insufficient muscle repair
- **Consequences**: No muscle growth, slow recovery
- **Solution**: 1.6-2.2g/kg body weight daily
- **Rule**: Protein at every meal

### Mistake 2: Extreme Caloric Deficit
- **Problem**: Too aggressive cutting
- **Consequences**: Muscle loss, metabolic adaptation
- **Solution**: Moderate deficit (300-500 calories)
- **Rule**: Slow and steady wins

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Carbs
- **Problem**: Low energy, poor performance
- **Consequences**: Fatigue, reduced training quality
- **Solution**: Adequate carbs for activity level
- **Rule**: Carbs are fuel, not the enemy

### Mistake 4: Not Tracking Calories
- **Problem**: Unaware of intake
- **Consequences**: Weight gain or loss not as intended
- **Solution**: Track for awareness (at least initially)
- **Rule**: You can't manage what you don't measure

### Mistake 5: Too Much Junk Food
- **Problem**: Poor nutrition quality
- **Consequences**: Fat gain, poor health, inflammation
- **Solution**: 80/20 rule (80% whole foods, 20% flexible)
- **Rule**: Nutrition is foundation

### Mistake 6: Not Drinking Enough Water
- **Problem**: Dehydration
- **Consequences**: Fatigue, poor performance, headaches
- **Solution**: 35-40ml per kg body weight daily
- **Rule**: Water is essential

### Mistake 7: Skipping Meals
- **Problem**: Irregular eating patterns
- **Consequences**: Overeating later, energy crashes
- **Solution**: Regular meals, plan ahead
- **Rule**: Consistency matters

## 3. Recovery Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Not Sleeping Enough
- **Problem**: Insufficient recovery
- **Consequences**: Fatigue, decreased performance, injuries
- **Solution**: 7-9 hours nightly
- **Rule**: Sleep is performance enhancer

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Soreness
- **Problem**: Training through pain
- **Consequences**: Injuries become chronic
- **Solution**: Address pain early, modify training
- **Rule**: Pain is signal, not weakness

### Mistake 3: No Deloads
- **Problem**: Fatigue accumulation
- **Consequences**: Overtraining, burnout
- **Solution**: Plan deloads every 4-6 weeks
- **Rule**: Deloads are part of training

### Mistake 4: Poor Stress Management
- **Problem**: High cortisol levels
- **Consequences**: Fat gain, poor recovery, sleep issues
- **Solution**: Meditation, deep breathing, hobbies
- **Rule**: Mental health is physical health

### Mistake 5: Ignoring Mobility
- **Problem**: Tightness, limited range of motion
- **Consequences**: Injuries, poor form
- **Solution**: Daily mobility work
- **Rule**: Move well before moving heavy

## 4. Mindset Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Comparing to Others
- **Problem**: Unrealistic expectations
- **Consequences**: Frustration, quitting
- **Solution**: Focus on personal progress
- **Rule**: Compare to yesterday's you

### Mistake 2: All-or-Nothing Thinking
- **Problem**: Perfectionism
- **Consequences**: Quitting after slip-ups
- **Solution**: Embrace consistency over perfection
- **Rule**: Progress, not perfection

### Mistake 3: Lack of Patience
- **Problem**: Expecting instant results
- **Consequences**: Frustration, quitting
- **Solution**: Set realistic timelines
- **Rule**: Fitness is a marathon, not sprint

### Mistake 4: No Goal Setting
- **Problem**: Directionless training
- **Consequences**: No motivation, no progress
- **Solution**: Set SMART goals
- **Rule**: Clear goals drive action

### Mistake 5: Fear of Failure
- **Problem**: Avoiding challenges
- **Consequences**: No growth
- **Solution**: Embrace failure as learning
- **Rule**: Growth happens outside comfort zone

## 5. Programming Mistakes

### Mistake 1: No Program
- **Problem**: Random workouts
- **Consequences**: No progress, wasted effort
- **Solution**: Follow structured program
- **Rule**: Plan your work, work your plan

### Mistake 2: Program Hopping
- **Problem**: Constantly changing programs
- **Consequences**: No adaptation, no progress
- **Solution**: Stick with program for 8-12 weeks
- **Rule**: Give programs time to work

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Weak Points
- **Problem**: Only training strengths
- **Consequences**: Imbalances, injuries
- **Solution**: Prioritize weak areas
- **Rule**: Train what you suck at

### Mistake 4: Too Much Variety
- **Problem**: No progressive overload
- **Consequences**: No adaptation
- **Solution**: Consistent exercises with progression
- **Rule**: Master basics first

### Mistake 5: No Tracking
- **Problem**: No data to make decisions
- **Consequences**: Can't measure progress
- **Solution**: Log workouts, track metrics
- **Rule**: What gets measured gets managed

## 6. Equipment Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Wrong Footwear
- **Problem**: Poor stability, increased injury risk
- **Consequences**: Knee, ankle, hip problems
- **Solution**: Proper shoes for activity
- **Rule**: Invest in good shoes

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Equipment Maintenance
- **Problem**: Unsafe equipment
- **Consequences**: Injuries, equipment failure
- **Solution**: Regular maintenance
- **Rule**: Well-maintained equipment is safe equipment

### Mistake 3: Using Wrong Equipment
- **Problem**: Ineffective training
- **Consequences**: No progress, frustration
- **Solution**: Learn proper equipment use
- **Rule**: Right tool for right job

## 7. Social Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Training Alone All the Time
- **Problem**: No motivation, no accountability
- **Consequences**: Inconsistent training
- **Solution**: Find training partner or community
- **Rule**: Accountability drives consistency

### Mistake 2: Comparing to Social Media
- **Problem**: Unrealistic expectations
- **Consequences**: Frustration, poor body image
- **Solution**: Focus on personal progress
- **Rule**: Social media is highlight reel

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Professional Advice
- **Problem**: Self-diagnosis, wrong training
- **Consequences**: Injuries, no progress
- **Solution**: Consult professionals when needed
- **Rule**: Experts save time and prevent injuries

## 8. How to Fix Mistakes

### Step 1: Identify
- What am I doing wrong?
- Why am I doing it?
- What are the consequences?

### Step 2: Plan
- What should I do instead?
- How will I implement change?
- What support do I need?

### Step 3: Act
- Implement change consistently
- Track progress
- Adjust as needed

### Step 4: Review
- What's working?
- What's not?
- What needs adjustment?
