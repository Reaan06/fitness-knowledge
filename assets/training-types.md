# Training Types — Complete Guide

## 1. Strength Training (Fuerza)

### Bodybuilding (Hipertrofia)
- **Goal**: Muscle size increase
- **Rep range**: 8-12 reps
- **Sets**: 3-5 per exercise
- **Rest**: 60-90 seconds
- **Tempo**: Controlled (3-1-2-0)
- **Frequency**: 4-6 days/week (split routines)

### Powerlifting (Potencia)
- **Goal**: Maximum strength
- **Rep range**: 1-5 reps
- **Sets**: 3-6 per exercise
- **Rest**: 3-5 minutes
- **Focus**: Squat, Bench Press, Deadlift
- **Frequency**: 3-4 days/week

### Strength Endurance
- **Goal**: Sustained strength over time
- **Rep range**: 12-20 reps
- **Sets**: 2-4 per exercise
- **Rest**: 30-60 seconds
- **Useful for**: Sports requiring repeated efforts

## 2. Cardiovascular Training (Cardio)

### Low-Intensity Steady State (LISS)
- **Intensity**: 50-65% max HR
- **Duration**: 30-60 minutes
- **Examples**: Walking, cycling, swimming
- **Benefits**: Fat oxidation, recovery, base endurance

### High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)
- **Intensity**: 80-95% max HR
- **Duration**: 15-30 minutes
- **Protocol**: 20-40s work / 10-20s rest (Tabata style)
- **Benefits**: EPOC, time efficiency, VO2max improvement

### Moderate-Intensity Continuous Training (MICT)
- **Intensity**: 65-80% max HR
- **Duration**: 20-60 minutes
- **Examples**: Jogging, cycling, rowing
- **Benefits**: Cardiovascular health, endurance

### Sprint Interval Training (SIT)
- **Intensity**: 100% max HR
- **Duration**: 10-30 minutes total
- **Protocol**: 30s all-out / 4-5 minutes rest
- **Benefits**: Maximum VO2max improvement

## 3. Functional Training

### Definition
Training that prepares the body for daily activities by improving movement patterns, not isolated muscles.

### Key Principles
- Multi-joint movements
- Core stability emphasis
- Balance and coordination
- Movement quality over quantity

### Common Patterns
- Push (push-ups, overhead press)
- Pull (rows, pull-ups)
- Squat (bodyweight squats, goblet squats)
- Hip hinge (deadlifts, hip thrusts)
- Lunge (forward, reverse, lateral)
- Rotation (wood chops, Russian twists)
- Carry (farmer's walk, suitcase carry)

## 4. Plyometrics (Pliometría)

### Definition
Explosive movements that use the stretch-shortening cycle.

### Categories
- **Low intensity**: Jump rope, box steps
- **Medium intensity**: Squat jumps, lunge jumps
- **High intensity**: Depth jumps, bounding

### Benefits
- Power development
- Speed improvement
- Bone density increase
- Athletic performance

## 5. Olympic Weightlifting (Halterofilia)

### Main Lifts
- **Snatch (Arrancada)**: Bar from ground to overhead in one movement
- **Clean & Jerk (Cargada y Envión)**: Bar from ground to shoulders, then overhead

### Benefits
- Full-body power
- Coordination
- Mobility
- Athletic performance

## 6. Calisthenics (Calistenia)

### Definition
Bodyweight training using gravity as resistance.

### Progressions
- **Beginner**: Wall push-ups, assisted squats
- **Intermediate**: Push-ups, pull-ups, dips
- **Advanced**: Muscle-ups, planche, front lever

### Benefits
- No equipment needed
- Functional strength
- Body control
- Progressive mastery

## 7. CrossFit

### Definition
Constantly varied functional movements performed at high intensity.

### Components
- Olympic weightlifting
- Powerlifting
- Gymnastics
- Cardio
- Plyometrics

### Benefits
- General fitness
- Community
- Competition
- Variety

## 8. Mobility & Flexibility

### Static Stretching
- Hold 15-60 seconds
- Post-workout preferred
- Improves flexibility

### Dynamic Stretching
- Movement-based
- Pre-workout preferred
- Improves range of motion

### PNF Stretching
- Contract-relax technique
- Most effective for flexibility
- Requires partner or equipment

### Yoga
- Combines flexibility, balance, mindfulness
- Various styles (Vinyasa, Hatha, Yin)
- Benefits mental and physical health

## 9. Sport-Specific Training

### Examples
- **Running**: Speed work, tempo runs, long runs
- **Swimming**: Technique drills, interval training
- **Martial Arts**: Technique, sparring, conditioning
- **Team Sports**: Agility, speed, power, endurance

## 10. Rehabilitation Training

### Principles
- Pain-free movements only
- Progressive overload
- Focus on weak points
- Professional guidance recommended

### Common Approaches
- Physical therapy exercises
- Corrective exercises
- Prehabilitation
- Return-to-sport protocols
