## Compound Exercises

### Barbell Back Squat
**Target Muscles**: Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings, Core
**Equipment**: Barbell, Squat Rack
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

**Starting Position:**
- Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
- Barbell resting on upper traps (not neck)
- Hands wider than shoulder-width on bar
- Chest up, core braced
- Feet slightly turned out (15-30 degrees)

**Movement:**
1. Take a deep breath and brace core
2. Initiate by pushing hips back and bending knees
3. Descend until thighs are parallel to floor (or below)
4. Keep chest up, knees tracking over toes
5. Drive through heels to stand up
6. Exhale at the top, squeeze glutes

**Common Mistakes:**
- Knees caving inward (valgus)
- Rounding lower back (butt wink)
- Rising onto toes
- Looking down

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "Imagine sitting back into a chair"
- "Keep your chest proud throughout"
- "Push the floor away with your feet"
- "Knees should track over your pinky toes"

### Deadlift (Conventional)
**Target Muscles**: Posterior Chain (Hamstrings, Glutes, Lower Back), Lats, Grip
**Equipment**: Barbell
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

**Starting Position:**
- Stand with feet hip-width apart
- Bar over mid-foot
- Bend at hips and knees to grip bar
- Hands shoulder-width apart
- Back flat, chest up
- Shoulders slightly in front of bar

**Movement:**
1. Take a deep breath, brace core
2. Push floor away with feet
3. Keep bar close to body
4. Stand up by extending hips and knees simultaneously
5. Lock out at top with shoulders back
6. Reverse movement to return bar to floor

**Common Mistakes:**
- Rounded lower back
- Bar drifting away from body
- Hyperextending at top
- Jerking the bar off floor

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "Think of this as a push, not a pull"
- "Keep the bar glued to your legs"
- "Squeeze your lats like you're holding oranges"
- "Drive through your heels"

### Bench Press (Barbell)
**Target Muscles**: Chest, Front Delts, Triceps
**Equipment**: Barbell, Bench
**Difficulty**: Beginner to Advanced

**Starting Position:**
- Lie on bench, eyes under bar
- Feet flat on floor
- Grip bar slightly wider than shoulder-width
- Retract shoulder blades, arch upper back
- Unrack bar with arms straight

**Movement:**
1. Lower bar to mid-chest with control
2. Keep elbows at 45-75 degree angle (not 90)
3. Touch chest lightly
4. Press bar back up to starting position
5. Exhale during press

**Common Mistakes:**
- Flared elbows (90 degrees)
- Bouncing bar off chest
- Lifting feet off floor
- Inconsistent bar path

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "Think about breaking the bar in half"
- "Lower to your nipple line"
- "Drive your feet into the floor"
- "Keep your shoulder blades in your back pockets"

### Overhead Press (Standing)
**Target Muscles**: Shoulders, Triceps, Upper Chest, Core
**Equipment**: Barbell
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

**Starting Position:**
- Stand with feet hip-width apart
- Bar resting on front delts (not collarbone)
- Hands just outside shoulder width
- Elbows slightly in front of bar
- Core braced, glutes squeezed

**Movement:**
1. Take a deep breath, brace core
2. Press bar straight up
3. Move head back slightly to clear bar path
4. Lock out overhead with bar over mid-foot
5. Lower bar back to starting position with control

**Common Mistakes:**
- Excessive back lean
- Pressing bar forward
- Not bracing core
- Incomplete lockout

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "Squeeze your glutes and brace your abs"
- "Think about pushing yourself away from the bar"
- "Keep the bar close to your face"
- "Lock out with biceps by ears"

## Isolation Exercises

### Dumbbell Bicep Curl
**Target Muscles**: Biceps
**Equipment**: Dumbbells
**Difficulty**: Beginner

**Starting Position:**
- Stand with feet hip-width apart
- Dumbbells at sides, palms facing forward
- Core braced, shoulders back

**Movement:**
1. Keep elbows close to body
2. Curl dumbbells up by flexing elbows
3. Squeeze biceps at top
4. Lower with control (2-3 seconds)
5. Avoid swinging or using momentum

**Common Mistakes:**
- Swinging body for momentum
- Moving elbows forward
- Not full range of motion
- Using too much weight

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "Imagine your elbows are glued to your sides"
- "Curl up, don't swing up"
- "Squeeze at the top like you're flexing"
- "Lower slowly like you're fighting gravity"

### Tricep Pushdown (Cable)
**Target Muscles**: Triceps
**Equipment**: Cable Machine
**Difficulty**: Beginner

**Starting Position:**
- Stand facing cable machine
- Grip bar or rope attachment
- Elbows at sides, bent 90 degrees
- Core braced

**Movement:**
1. Keep elbows stationary at sides
2. Push down until arms are fully extended
3. Squeeze triceps at bottom
4. Return to starting position with control
5. Avoid using body weight

**Common Mistakes:**
- Elbows moving forward/back
- Leaning body weight
- Not full extension
- Using too much weight

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "Elbows stay glued to your ribs"
- "Think about straightening your arms, not pushing down"
- "Squeeze like you're trying to touch your forearms together"
- "Control the weight back up"

### Lateral Raise (Dumbbell)
**Target Muscles**: Medial Deltoids
**Equipment**: Dumbbells
** Difficulty**: Beginner to Intermediate

**Starting Position:**
- Stand with feet hip-width apart
- Dumbbells at sides, palms facing body
- Slight bend in elbows
- Core braced

**Movement:**
1. Raise dumbbells out to sides
2. Keep slight bend in elbows
3. Raise until arms parallel to floor (or slightly above)
4. Lead with elbows, not hands
5. Lower with control

**Common Mistakes:**
- Using momentum/swinging
- Raising arms too high
- Shrugging shoulders
- Using too much weight

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "Imagine pouring two glasses of water"
- "Lead with your elbows, not your hands"
- "Raise until you look like a 'T'"
- "Control the negative"

## Bodyweight Exercises

### Push-Up
**Target Muscles**: Chest, Shoulders, Triceps, Core
**Equipment**: None
**Difficulty**: Beginner to Intermediate

**Starting Position:**
- Hands slightly wider than shoulder-width
- Body in straight line from head to heels
- Core engaged, glutes squeezed
- Head neutral (looking at floor)

**Movement:**
1. Lower body by bending elbows
2. Keep elbows at 45-degree angle
3. Lower until chest nears floor
4. Push back up to starting position
5. Maintain body alignment throughout

**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips sagging or piking
- Flared elbows (90 degrees)
- Not full range of motion
- Head dropping

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "Imagine a stick along your spine - don't break it"
- "Elbows at 45 degrees, not 90"
- "Touch your chest to the floor"
- "Look at a spot about a foot in front of your hands"

---

### Pull-Up
**Target Muscles**: Lats, Biceps, Rear Delts, Core
**Equipment**: Pull-Up Bar
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

**Starting Position:**
- Hang from bar with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width
- Palms facing away (overhand grip)
- Arms fully extended
- Core engaged

**Movement:**
1. Initiate by depressing shoulder blades
2. Pull up by driving elbows down and back
3. Continue until chin clears bar
4. Lower with control to full extension
5. Avoid kipping or swinging

**Common Mistakes:**
- Swinging/kipping
- Not full range of motion
- Shrugging shoulders
- Using momentum

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "Think about pulling your elbows to your pockets"
- "Get your chin over the bar"
- "Start from a dead hang"
- "Control the negative - don't just drop"

---

### Plank
**Target Muscles**: Core (Rectus Abdominis, Transverse Abdominis, Obliques)
**Equipment**: None
**Difficulty**: Beginner

**Starting Position:**
- Forearms on floor, elbows under shoulders
- Body in straight line from head to heels
- Core engaged, glutes squeezed
- Neutral head position

**Movement:**
1. Hold position for time
2. Maintain neutral spine
3. Breathe normally
4. Keep core braced throughout

**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips sagging
- Hips too high
- Holding breath
- Looking up

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "Imagine balancing a glass of water on your back"
- "Squeeze your glutes and abs"
- "Don't let your hips sag"
- "Breathe normally - don't hold your breath"

## Olympic Lifts

### Clean and Jerk
**Target Muscles**: Full Body (Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes, Back, Shoulders, Core)
**Equipment**: Barbell
**Difficulty**: Advanced

**Starting Position:**
- Feet hip-width apart
- Bar over mid-foot
- Hook grip on bar
- Hips lower than shoulders
- Back flat, chest up

**Clean Phase:**
1. Lift bar from floor with control
2. Explosively extend hips, knees, ankles (triple extension)
3. Pull body under bar into front squat position
4. Catch bar on front delts (rack position)
5. Stand up from squat

**Jerk Phase:**
1. Dip slightly by bending knees
2. Drive bar up with leg power
3. Split or push under bar
4. Lock out arms overhead
5. Recover to standing position

**Common Mistakes:**
- Pulling with arms too early
- Not extending fully
- Slow under the bar
- Poor rack position

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "This is a leg exercise, not an arm exercise"
- "Triple extend - hips, knees, ankles"
- "Get under the bar fast"
- "Stand up strong from the squat"

---

### Snatch
**Target Muscles**: Full Body (Emphasis on posterior chain, shoulders, core)
**Equipment**: Barbell
**Difficulty**: Advanced

**Starting Position:**
- Feet hip-width apart
- Wide grip on bar
- Hips lower than shoulders
- Back flat, chest up

**Movement:**
1. Lift bar from floor with control
2. Explosively extend hips, knees, ankles
3. Pull body under bar into overhead squat
4. Catch bar overhead with locked arms
5. Stand up from squat

**Common Mistakes:**
- Pulling with arms too early
- Not extending fully
- Slow under the bar
- Poor overhead position

**Cues for AI to Give Users:**
- "This is a leg exercise, not an arm exercise"
- "Triple extend - hips, knees, ankles"
- "Get under the bar fast"
- "Lock out overhead - fight for it"

## AI Integration Notes

### How to Use This Module
1. When user asks about an exercise, load this module
2. Describe the exercise step-by-step
3. Provide visual cues the user can imagine
4. Address common mistakes proactively
5. Offer modifications for fitness level

### Describing Form to Users
- Use analogies and metaphors
- Break movement into phases
- Highlight key checkpoints
- Address safety concerns first
- Offer regressions for beginners

### Safety Considerations
- Always recommend proper warm-up
- Suggest starting with lighter weight
- Recommend professional instruction for complex lifts
- Address individual limitations
- Provide alternatives for injuries/limitations
