## 1. Running

### 1.1 Key Physical Demands

| Distance | Primary Energy System | Key Physiological Demands |
|----------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| 5K | Aerobic (85%) + Anaerobic (15%) | VO2max ~85% utilization, lactate threshold ~90% VO2max |
| 10K | Aerobic (90%) + Anaerobic (10%) | High lactate threshold, sustainable pace ~88% VO2max |
| Half Marathon | Aerobic (95%) + Anaerobic (5%) | Lactate threshold pace, fat oxidation efficiency |
| Marathon | Aerobic (98%) + Anaerobic (2%) | Glycogen management, fat oxidation, economy |
| Trail/Ultra | Aerobic (99%+) | Time on feet, terrain adaptation, fueling logistics |

### 1.2 Training Programs

#### Base Building Phase (8–16 weeks)
- **Volume**: Start at current sustainable mileage, increase 10% per week
- **Intensity**: Zone 2 (conversational pace, ~65–75% max HR)
- **Frequency**: 4–6 runs per week
- **Key Session**: Long run — 25–35% of weekly volume
- **Strength**: 2×/week full body, focus on posterior chain

#### Marathon-Specific 16-Week Block Periodization

| Phase | Duration | Focus | Key Workouts |
|-------|----------|-------|-------------|
| Base | Weeks 1–4 | Aerobic foundation | Long runs, easy pace, strides |
| Build | Weeks 5–10 | Lactate threshold + volume | Tempo runs, marathon pace intervals |
| Peak | Weeks 11–14 | Race-specific | Long runs at MP, Yasso 800s |
| Taper | Weeks 15–16 | Freshness | Volume ↓ 40–60%, intensity maintained |

**Intensity Distribution Models**:
- **Pyramidal**: ~80% Zone 1, ~15% Zone 2, ~5% Zone 3 (most common in analyzed marathon plans)
- **Polarized**: ~80% Zone 1, ~20% Zone 3 (better for well-trained athletes per intervention research)
- **Emerging evidence**: 16-week pyramidal followed by 16-week polarized may yield greatest improvements

#### Sample Marathon Week (Peak Phase)

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Monday | Rest or easy cross-training |
| Tuesday | Intervals: 6×800m @ 5K pace, 400m jog recovery |
| Wednesday | Easy run 8–10 km + strides |
| Thursday | Tempo: 6–8 km @ half marathon pace |
| Friday | Rest or easy 5 km |
| Saturday | Long run: 28–32 km (last 8–10 km at marathon pace) |
| Sunday | Recovery run 5–6 km |

#### Trail/Ultra Running

- **Reverse periodization** often used: start with high intensity, build volume toward race
- **Back-to-back long runs**: Saturday long + Sunday moderate long (builds time-on-feet durability)
- **Vertical gain**: progressively match race elevation profile
- **Key sessions**: sustained climbs (30–60 min), downhill technique drills, mixed terrain
- **Typical preparation window**: 4–6 months for 50K, 6+ months for 100-mile

### 1.3 Periodization for Competition

- **Taper**: 2–3 weeks before race
  - Volume reduction: 41–60% in exponential pattern
  - Intensity: maintained (do NOT reduce)
  - Last week: ~50% further volume reduction from prior week
  - 3-week taper associated with better marathon performance (158,000-runner study)

- **Peak week**: 3–4 weeks out from race (timing confirmed across 92 analyzed plans)

### 1.4 Nutrition Considerations

| Training Phase | Carbohydrate | Protein | Fat |
|---------------|-------------|---------|-----|
| Base (moderate volume) | 5–7 g/kg/day | 1.4–1.6 g/kg/day | 1.0–1.2 g/kg/day |
| Peak (high volume) | 7–10 g/kg/day | 1.6–2.0 g/kg/day | 1.0–1.5 g/kg/day |
| Ultra training | 5–8 g/kg/day (periodized) | 1.6–2.5 g/kg/day | 1.0–2.0 g/kg/day |

- **Race fueling**: 30–90 g CHO/hour (distance-dependent)
- **Ultra racing**: 150–400 kcal/hour, 30–50 g CHO + 5–10 g protein
- **Hydration**: 450–750 mL/hour, sodium 500–1000 mg/L in hot conditions

### 1.5 Injury Prevention

- **Most common**: Runner's knee (PFPS), IT band syndrome, Achilles tendinopathy, shin splints, plantar fasciitis
- **Strength priorities**: single-leg work, eccentric calf (Alfredson protocol), Nordic hamstrings, hip abductor strength
- **Load management**: 10% rule, cut volume during pain, address gait mechanics
- **Shoe rotation**: alternate shoes with different drop/cushioning
- **Surface variation**: avoid 100% pavement

### 1.6 Recovery Strategies

- Immediate post-long-run: CHO + protein within 30 minutes (3:1 or 4:1 ratio)
- Contrast baths, compression, elevation for acute recovery
- Sleep extension to 8–10 hours during peak training
- Active recovery: 20–30 min easy walking, cycling, or swimming on rest days

## 2. Swimming

### 2.1 Key Physical Demands

| Distance | Primary Energy System | Stroke Rate | Key Demands |
|----------|----------------------|-------------|-------------|
| 50m (sprint) | Anaerobic alactic (50%) + lactic (50%) | High cadence | Power off blocks/turns, explosive start |
| 100m | Anaerobic (60%) + Aerobic (40%) | Moderate-high | Lactate tolerance, turn efficiency |
| 200m | Mixed (50/50) | Moderate | Pacing, stroke efficiency, lactate clearance |
| 400m+ | Aerobic (70–80%) + Anaerobic | Lower | Aerobic power, technique maintenance |
| 1500m/open water | Aerobic (85%+) | Sustainable | Pacing, sighting, mental endurance |

### 2.2 Training Programs

#### Annual Macrocycle Structure (52 weeks)

| Phase | Duration | Volume | Focus |
|-------|----------|--------|-------|
| Base I | 8–16 weeks | 50–70 km/wk | Aerobic foundation, technique volume |
| Base II | 6–10 weeks | 55–65 km/wk | Adding pool strength (paddles), introducing race pace |
| Specificity | 6–10 weeks | 40–55 km/wk | Race pace sets, lactate threshold, VO2max |
| Taper | 10–21 days | ↓40–60% | Freshness, intensity maintained |
| Active Rest | 1–2 weeks | Minimal | Recovery, cross-training |

**Load Pattern**: 3 weeks progressive loading (+10–15% volume) + 1 unload week (30–40% reduction)

#### Training Zones (Blood Lactate Based)

| Zone | Lactate (mmol/L) | HR | Training Effect | Sample Sets |
|------|------------------|-----|----------------|-------------|
| Z1 | ~2 | 30–40 bpm below max | Aerobic endurance, fat oxidation | 200–1500m repeats, continuous swim |
| Z2 | 2–4 | 20–30 bpm below max | Lactate threshold pace | 3000–4500m sets, 50–400m repeats |
| Z3 | 4–6 | 10–20 bpm below max | VO2max | 300–500m sets, 50–150m repeats |
| Z4 | 6–10 | Near max | Anaerobic capacity, speed endurance | 25–100m all-out, broken swims |
| Z5 | >10 | Max | Sprint speed, alactic power | 15–25m sprints, starts/turns |

#### Sample Elite 400m IM Week (Specificity Phase, ~65 km)

| Day | AM Session | PM Session |
|-----|-----------|-----------|
| Monday | Aerobic: 5 km (Z1–Z2), mixed strokes | Strength: max strength focus |
| Tuesday | VO2max: 12×200m @ Z3, 45" rest | Technique drill set + kick work |
| Wednesday | Aerobic: 6 km continuous, front crawl emphasis | Strength: power + speed endurance |
| Thursday | Lactate tolerance: 8×100m @ Z4, 3' rest | Recovery swim + core |
| Friday | Race pace: broken 400m IM (4×100 with 10" rest) | Strength: maximal strength |
| Saturday | Long aerobic: 8 km mixed | — |
| Sunday | Active recovery | — |

### 2.3 Dryland / Strength Training

| Phase | Focus | Sets × Reps | Exercises |
|-------|-------|-------------|-----------|
| General prep (base) | Strength + hypertrophy | 2–3 × 8–12 | Leg press, DB row, chin-ups, lunges, bench press |
| Specific prep | Max strength + power | 3–5 × 4–6 | Squat, pull-ups, hamstring curls, lat pulldowns |
| Competition phase | Power maintenance | 2–3 × 3–5 | Olympic lift variations, plyometrics, circuit |

### 2.4 Nutrition

- **Training nutrition**: CHO 5–8 g/kg/day, protein 1.4–1.8 g/kg/day
- **Between-session recovery for double days**: immediate CHO + protein
- **Pre-competition**: CHO loading 48–72 hours pre-race, familiar foods
- **Hydration**: 500–1000 mL/hour of training, electrolyte monitoring for pool sweating
- **Open water considerations**: increased fueling for cold-water metabolism, pre-cooling for warm water

### 2.5 Injury Prevention

- **Most common**: swimmer's shoulder (impingement, rotator cuff), patellar tendinopathy (breaststroke knee), lower back pain
- **Prevention**: rotator cuff prehab (2–3×/week 15–20 min), scapular stability, lat flexibility
- **Shoulder prevention protocol**: ER/IR with bands, YTWL exercises, sleeper stretches
- **Core work**: essential for body position, 3×/week minimum
- **Technique emphasis**: high elbow catch, proper breathing mechanics reduce shoulder stress

### 2.6 Recovery

- **Swimdown**: active recovery until lactate <2.5 mmol/L after hard sets
- **Periodization**: 3:1 load:unload, max 2 VO2max sessions per week
- **Taper protocol**: 10–21 days, volume ↓ 40–60%, intensity maintained (critical)
- **Sleep**: minimum 9–10 hours for high-volume training adolescents/elites

## 3. Cycling

### 3.1 Key Physical Demands

| Discipline | Primary Demands | Duration | Key Metric |
|------------|----------------|----------|------------|
| Road racing | Sustained power, surges, group tactics | 3–6 hours | FTP, W/kg |
| Criterium | Repeated high-intensity efforts, cornering | 45–90 min | Peak power, repeat sprint |
| Time trial | Steady submaximal power, aero position | 30–90 min | FTP, CdA efficiency |
| Mountain bike | Variable power, bike-handling, climbing | 1.5–4 hours | Short-term power + endurance |
| Gran fondo/century | Steady endurance, nutrition management | 4–8 hours | Fat oxidation, pacing |

### 3.2 Training Programs

#### Road Cycling Periodization (22–Week Plan)

| Phase | Duration | Focus | Key Sessions |
|-------|----------|-------|-------------|
| Base 1–3 | 8–12 weeks | Aerobic endurance, muscular endurance | Long zone 2 rides 3–5 hours |
| Build (VO2max) | 6–8 weeks | FTP improvement, threshold | Sweet spot intervals, VO2max work |
| Peak | 2–3 weeks | Race simulation, specificity | Group ride simulation, crit practice |
| Taper | 1–2 weeks | Freshness, neuromuscular priming | Short sharp efforts, ↓ volume 40–50% |

**Intensity Distribution**: ~80% Zone 1–2 (easy), ~20% Zone 4+ (hard), minimal Zone 3 (tempo)

#### Sample Road Cycling Week (Build Phase, 12–15 hours)

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Monday | Rest or active recovery (1 hour easy spin) |
| Tuesday | Sweet spot: 3×20 min @ 85–88% FTP, 8 min rest |
| Wednesday | Endurance: 2.5–3 hours Zone 2 |
| Thursday | Threshold: 2×45 min @ 72% FTP (race pace) |
| Friday | Recovery: 1 hour easy spin |
| Saturday | Long ride: 4–6 hours Zone 2, with race-pace blocks |
| Sunday | Recovery ride 1.5 hours or brick run off bike |

#### Mountain Bike Specifics

- **More variable power demands** than road cycling
- **Shorter, punchier intervals**: 2–5 min at 100–120% FTP with technical sections
- **Bike handling sessions**: 1×/week minimum, skills practice on technical terrain
- **Strength focus**: upper body, core, and grip endurance (not just legs)
- **Typical peak volume**: 10–14 hours/week for age-group MTB racers

### 3.3 Nutrition for Cycling

| Ride Duration | CHO/Hour | Hydration | Notes |
|--------------|----------|-----------|-------|
| <90 min | Water only | 500–750 mL | No fueling needed |
| 1.5–3 hours | 30–60 g | 500–750 mL | Begin fueling at minute 20 |
| 3–5 hours | 60–90 g | 500–750 mL | Mixed glucose + fructose (1:0.8 ratio) |
| 5+ hours | 90–120 g | 500–750 mL | Trained gut required, practice in base phase |

- **Caffeine**: 2–3 mg/kg in final 90 minutes for race-day performance
- **Sodium**: 500–1000 mg/hour on long or hot rides
- **Pre-ride**: carb-rich meal 2–3 hours before
- **Post-ride**: CHO + protein within 30 minutes

### 3.4 Injury Prevention

- **Most common**: patellar tendinopathy, IT band syndrome, lower back pain, neck pain, ulnar nerve compression
- **Bike fit**: single most important prevention measure
- **Strength priorities**: core stability (anti-extension, anti-rotation), hip stability, scapular retraction
- **Position variation**: stand periodically on long rides, change hand positions
- **Cervical spine**: posterior neck isometrics, chin tucks

### 3.5 Recovery

- **Active recovery**: 30–60 min easy spin on rest days
- **Post-ride nutrition**: immediate window critical for multi-day events
- **Sleep**: 8–9 hours minimum during high-volume blocks
- **Massage/foam rolling**: prioritize quads, hamstrings, glutes, IT band
- **Periodization**: 3:1 or 4:1 weekly load pattern

## 4. Triathlon

### 4.1 Key Physical Demands

| Distance | Swim | Bike | Run | Total Time (approx) |
|----------|------|------|-----|---------------------|
| Sprint | 750m | 20 km | 5 km | 1–1.5 hours |
| Olympic | 1.5 km | 40 km | 10 km | 2–3.5 hours |
| Half Ironman | 1.9 km | 90 km | 21.1 km | 4.5–6.5 hours |
| Full Ironman | 3.8 km | 180 km | 42.2 km | 9–17 hours |

### 4.2 Training Programs

#### Full Ironman: 16-Week Age-Group Build

| Phase | Duration | Hours/Week | Focus |
|-------|----------|------------|-------|
| Base endurance | Weeks 1–6 | 8–12 | Long rides, aerobic runs, technique |
| Race-specific power | Weeks 7–12 | 12–15 | Sweet spot intervals, brick sessions |
| Peak | Weeks 13–14 | 14–15 | Highest volume, specificity |
| Taper | Weeks 15–16 | 6–8 | Volume ↓ 40–60%, intensity maintained |

#### Sample Ironman Build Week (12–15 hours total)

| Day | AM | PM |
|-----|-----|-----|
| Monday | Rest | — |
| Tuesday | Swim: 3 km aerobic + drills | Bike: 90 min sweet spot (3×20 @ 85% FTP) |
| Wednesday | Run: 60 min tempo | Strength: 45 min |
| Thursday | Swim: 2.5 km pace work | Bike: 75 min race-pace (2×45 min @ 72% FTP) |
| Friday | Run: 45 min easy | Rest |
| Saturday | Long ride: 4.5–6 hours Zone 2 | Brick run: 15–30 min off bike |
| Sunday | Long run: 2–2.5 hours Zone 2 | Swim: 2 km easy recovery |

**Key Training Principles**:
- **Brick sessions**: 4–6 across the plan, mostly in Phase 2
- **Long ride fueling**: practice exact race nutrition (80–120 g CHO/hour)
- **Bike pacing for race day**: 65–72% FTP for age-group athletes
- **The bike-run handoff**: run is fueled 60% by what was eaten on the bike

### 4.3 Periodization Models

- **Traditional**: Base (high volume, low intensity) → Build (increasing intensity) → Peak (specificity) → Taper
- **Reverse periodization**: Start with higher intensity, build volume toward race (used by some elite ultra/trail athletes)
- **Polarized training**: 80% Zone 1, 20% Zone 3 — supported by research for well-trained triathletes

### 4.4 Nutrition

- **Full Ironman bike**: 90–100 g CHO/hour (glucose:fructose 1:0.8)
- **Run fueling**: gels, sport drinks, aid station planning
- **Hydration**: 500–750 mL/hour, sodium 500–1000 mg/hour
- **Pre-race**: CHO loading 2–3 days out, 7–10 g/kg/day
- **Gut training**: essential — practice race nutrition in training, start at 60 g/hour in base phase

### 4.5 Injury Prevention

- **Most common**: runner's knee, IT band syndrome, swimmer's shoulder, cyclist's back pain
- **Cross-training**: inherent in triathlon (cross-training = other disciplines)
- **Strength priorities**: glute med, posterior chain, scapular stability, rotator cuff
- **Transition practice**: reduces fall/injury risk in T1/T2
- **Load management**: total weekly load across 3 disciplines

### 4.6 Recovery

- **Post-long-session**: immediate CHO + protein
- **Sleep**: 8–10 hours during peak training
- **Compression**: useful for travel and post-hard sessions
- **Active recovery**: easy cross-training days
- **Period deload**: 1 lighter week every 3–4 weeks

## 5. Team Sports

### 5.1 Key Physical Demands

| Sport | Movement Pattern | Energy System | Key Qualities |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|---------------|
| Soccer | Multi-directional, repeated sprints | Aerobic (70%) + Anaerobic (30%) | VO2max, repeat sprint ability, agility |
| Basketball | Vertical + lateral, frequent direction change | Anaerobic-alactic dominant | Jump height, lateral speed, change of direction |
| American Football | Short bursts (5–10 sec), power | ATP-PCr primary | Maximal strength, power, speed |
| Volleyball | Vertical jumps, short explosive rallies | ATP-PCr + aerobic recovery | Vertical jump, approach speed, reactive agility |

### 5.2 Training Programs

#### Off-Season 12-Week Multi-Sport S&C (Mike Boyle Model)

| Phase | Duration | Focus | Key Methods |
|-------|----------|-------|-------------|
| Accumulation | Weeks 1–4 | Work capacity, foundational strength | 3–4×/week, moderate loads, higher volume |
| Intensification | Weeks 5–8 | Max strength, power development | Lower reps, higher intensity, plyometrics |
| Realization | Weeks 9–12 | Speed, sport-specific power | Low volume, high velocity, sport simulation |

**Weekly Split**: 4 days/week
- Day 1: Lower body strength + core
- Day 2: Upper body strength + plyometrics
- Day 3: Full body power + speed work
- Day 4: Conditioning + injury prevention

#### In-Season Maintenance (2×/Week)

- **Frequency**: 2 sessions per week (maintains strength, avoids fatigue)
- **Structure**: Full body, compound movements, RPE 7–8
- **Volume**: 3–4 sets of 5–8 reps
- **Training before practice**: strength work before sport practice to reduce interference
- **Emphasis**: explosive movements, injury prevention (hamstrings, core, ankles)

#### Sample Soccer Off-Season Week (5-Day Model)

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Monday | Lower body strength (squat 4×6, RDL 3×8, lunges 3×8 + plyometrics) |
| Tuesday | Linear speed + conditioning (40m sprints, change of direction drills) |
| Wednesday | Upper body strength (bench 4×6, rows 4×8, overhead press 3×8) |
| Thursday | Agility + sport-specific conditioning (pro-agility, COD drills) |
| Friday | Full body power (clean variations, jumps, med ball throws) |

### 5.3 Periodization for Team Sports

- **Off-season (8–12 weeks)**: highest volume, build GPP, strength focus
- **Pre-season (4–6 weeks)**: shift to sport-specific conditioning, tactical integration
- **In-season (24–36 weeks)**: maintenance strength (2×/week), skill + tactic priority
- **Post-season (2–4 weeks)**: active rest, cross-training, psychological break

### 5.4 Nutrition Considerations

| Phase | CHO | Protein | Fat | Notes |
|-------|-----|---------|-----|-------|
| Off-season (heavy training) | 5–7 g/kg | 1.6–2.2 g/kg | 1.0–1.5 g/kg | Muscle gain focus |
| In-season (practice + games) | 6–8 g/kg | 1.6–2.0 g/kg | 0.8–1.2 g/kg | Pre-game fueling critical |
| Game day | 1–4 g/kg pre-game | Moderate | Low | 3–4 hours pre-game meal |

- **Pre-game**: 3–4 hours before, moderate CHO, lean protein, low fat
- **Halftime/sideline**: easily digestible CHO (banana, sports drink, gel)
- **Post-game**: immediate CHO + protein for glycogen resynthesis

### 5.5 Injury Prevention

| Sport | Most Common Injuries | Prevention Priorities |
|-------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Soccer | Hamstring strain, ACL, ankle sprain | Nordic curls, single-leg stability, eccentric quad |
| Basketball | Ankle sprain, patellar tendinopathy, ACL | Balance training, landing mechanics, hip/core |
| Football | ACL, concussion, shoulder dislocation | Neck strengthening, tackle technique, ACL prevention |
| Volleyball | Ankle sprain, patellar tendinopathy, shoulder | Landing mechanics, rotator cuff, jump technique |

**ACL Prevention Programs** (FIFA 11+, PEP Program):
- Proximal hamstring strength
- Single-leg squat/landing control
- Core stability with anti-rotation
- Neuromuscular training before practice

### 5.6 Recovery

- **Between games**: 48–72 hours minimum between full-intensity matches
- **Post-game protocol**: rehydration, carb + protein within 30 min, compression, sleep
- **In-season deload**: 1 light training day per week, scheduled rest days
- **Travel recovery**: sleep hygiene, hydration management, compression on flights

## 6. Combat Sports

### 6.1 Key Physical Demands

| Sport | Round Duration | Work-to-Rest | Primary Energy System | Unique Demands |
|-------|---------------|--------------|----------------------|----------------|
| Boxing | 3 min × 3–12 rounds | 1 min rest | Aerobic (70–80%) + anaerobic | Head movement, neck strength, rotational power |
| MMA | 5 min × 3–5 rounds | 1 min rest | Aerobic (70–80%) + anaerobic (20–30%) | Multi-modal, grip strength, clinch, ground work |
| BJJ | 5–10 min continuous | No rest | Aerobic (75–85%) | Grip endurance, isometric strength, flexibility |

### 6.2 Training Programs

#### 13-Week MMA Fight Camp Periodization

| Phase | Duration | Focus | Strength | Conditioning | Skill |
|-------|----------|-------|----------|-------------|-------|
| General Prep | 5 weeks | Foundation | Hypertrophy + max strength (3–4×/wk) | LIIT (Zone 2, 30–40 min) | Moderate volume, technique focus |
| Specific Prep | 4 weeks | Conversion | Strength → power (2–3×/wk) | HIIT (round-specific intervals) | High volume, sparring, tactical |
| Competition | 2 weeks | Peaking | Neural priming (1–2×/wk, low vol) | Sport-specific (sparring) | Peak sharpness |
| Taper | 2 weeks | Recovery | 1 session at opener weight | Light activity | Drills only |

**Periodization Models**:
- **Block periodization**: 3–4 week blocks, dominant quality per block (strength → power → conditioning)
- **Concurrent**: maintain all qualities with varied emphasis week-to-week (better for annual readiness)
- **Linear periodization**: off-season (hypertrophy → strength → power), fight camp (conditioning emphasis)

#### Sample Strength Block (Off-Season, 2 Sessions/Week)

| Day A | Day B |
|-------|-------|
| Deadlift 4×5 @ 75–80% | Squat 4×5 @ 75–80% |
| Push press 4×5 | DB Row 4×8 |
| Weighted pull-up 4×6 | Bench/dip 4×6 |
| Ab wheel 3×10 | Reverse hyper 3×12 |
| Conditioning: Zone 2 30–40 min | Conditioning: Zone 2 30–40 min |

#### Sample Fight Camp Conditioning Week

| Day | AM | PM |
|-----|-----|-----|
| Monday | Power: clean 5×3 @ 70% | Skill: boxing + wrestling 90 min |
| Tuesday | HIIT: 5×3 min rounds @ 85% (1 min rest) | Skill: BJJ + muay Thai 90 min |
| Wednesday | Strength maintenance: 3×3 @ 85% | Skill: sparring 60 min |
| Thursday | HIIT: 8×30 sec all-out (90 sec rest) | Skill: technique + drilling 90 min |
| Friday | Power: plyometrics, med ball throws | Skill: full sparring 45 min |
| Saturday | Active recovery: swim 30 min | Light drilling technique |
| Sunday | Rest | — |

### 6.3 Key Strength Exercises for Combat

| Movement | Why | Prescription |
|----------|-----|-------------|
| Deadlift | Posterior chain, takedown/clinch power | 4×5 @ 75–85% |
| Squat/Trap bar deadlift | Quad-dominant power, shot defense | 4×5 @ 75–80% |
| Push press | Integrated power, clinch control | 4×5 @ 70–80% |
| Weighted pull-up | Takedown, guard retention, grip | 4×6 @ bodyweight +10–30% |
| Neck harness | Concussion resilience, clinch | 3×15–20, 2–3×/week |
| Farmer carries | Grip endurance, overall stability | 3×40m heavy |

**Pull-to-push ratio**: 2:1 recommended (combat athletes overdevelop pushing patterns)

### 6.4 Nutrition

| Phase | Caloric Focus | CHO | Protein |
|-------|--------------|-----|---------|
| Off-season strength block | Maintenance to slight surplus | 4–5 g/kg | 1.6–2.0 g/kg |
| Fight camp conditioning | Slight deficit for weight class | 3–4 g/kg | 1.8–2.2 g/kg |
| Weigh-in week | Strategic water + carb manipulation | Variable | Maintain high protein |
| Post-weigh-in | Rehydration + glycogen loading | 7–10 g/kg | 1.6–2.0 g/kg |

- **Weight cutting**: 2% water loss = measurable power reduction; ≥3% needs minimum 2 hours rehydration
- **NEVER** experiment with weight cuts during fight week — test 4+ weeks out

### 6.5 Injury Prevention

- **Neck strength**: directly correlated with concussion resistance — minimum 3×15–20, 2–3×/week
- **Shoulder health**: combat athletes overdevelop pushing — face pulls, band pull-aparts every session
- **Hand/wrist**: knuckle conditioning, wrist mobility, proper wrapping technique
- **Knee**: ACL prevention work (single-leg landing, lateral stability)
- **Overuse monitoring**: 30:30 interval sessions (30 sec max effort, 30 sec rest) limit to 10–16 intervals max

### 6.6 Recovery

- **Between high-intensity sessions**: minimum 48 hours for full CNS recovery
- **Deload**: 1 week every 4th week, reduce volume 40–50%
- **Readiness monitoring**: daily wellness score <6/10 = reduce intensity
- **Sleep**: 8–10 hours during fight camp
- **Active recovery**: zone 2 cardio, light swimming, mobility work

## 7. Martial Arts (Karate, Taekwondo, Judo)

### 7.1 Key Physical Demands

| Sport | Match Duration | Key Qualities | Energy System |
|-------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Karate (kumite) | 3 min | Speed, timing, explosive power, reaction time | ATP-PCr + aerobic |
| Karate (kata) | 2–4 min performance | Power, precision, expression, breathing control | Mixed |
| Taekwondo | 3×2 min rounds | Kicking speed, flexibility, agility, reaction | Aerobic base + explosive |
| Judo | 4–5 min | Explosive power, grip strength, anaerobic endurance | Anaerobic lactic dominant |

### 7.2 Training Programs

#### Block Periodization for Olympic Taekwondo (ATR Model)

| Phase | Duration | Focus | Key Methods |
|-------|----------|-------|-------------|
| **Accumulation** | 4–5 weeks | Basic skills, aerobic base, general strength | Technical drills, light sparring, circuit training, 4–6 exercises × 3–4 sets × 8–12 reps |
| **Transmutation** | 4–5 weeks | Power conversion, speed development | Plyometrics (40+ jumps/session, 3×/week), sport-specific power drills, interval training |
| **Realization** | 2–3 weeks | Peaking, competition simulation | Technical speed work, reaction drills, sparring scenarios, ↓ volume ↑ intensity |

#### Judo Periodization (Double Periodization Model)

| Period | Duration | Volume | Intensity | Focus |
|--------|----------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Preparation I | 8–12 weeks | High ↑ | Moderate → high | GPP, randori, aerobic base |
| Competition I | 4–6 weeks | Moderate | High | Specific preparation, peak for event |
| Preparation II | 6–8 weeks | High | Moderate → high | Rebuild, address weaknesses |
| Competition II | 4–6 weeks | Moderate | High | Peak for major championship |
| Transition | 2–4 weeks | Low | Low | Active recovery |

**Judo-specific training volume**:
- Advanced/elite: 9–15 sessions/week (on-mat + S&C)
- 65–70% tachi-waza (standing), 30–35% ne-waza (ground)
- Randori at 80%+ MHR minimum 180 hours/year
- Minimum 10 tournaments (40 bouts) per year for elite pathway

#### Sample Judo Elite Week (Competition Period)

| Phase | Calories | Protein | CHO | Fat |
|-------|----------|---------|-----|-----|
| Hypertrophy | Maintenance to slight surplus | 1.6–2.2 g/kg | 4–6 g/kg | 0.8–1.2 g/kg |

| Peaking | Maintenance | 1.6–2.0 g/kg | 5–6 g/kg | 0.6–0.8 g/kg |
| Meet week (not cutting) | Maintenance | 1.6–2.0 g/kg | 7–10 g/kg (load) | 0.6–0.8 g/kg |

**Meet Week Strategy**:
- 2–3 days pre-meet: increase CHO to 7–10 g/kg (glycogen loading)
- 24 hours out: reduce fiber to minimize GI discomfort
- Competition day: familiar foods, moderate CHO + lean protein, low fiber
- **Water cutting**: each 2% body weight lost reduces power output — minimize or avoid

### 10.4 Recovery

- **Deload**: every 4–6 weeks, reduce sets by 40–60%, maintain intensity

### 10.5 Injury Prevention

- **Most common**: low back (deadlift/squat), shoulder (bench), patellar tendinopathy (squat)
- **Prevention priorities**:
  - Thoracic spine mobility (for overhead and squat depth)
  - Hip capsule mobility (squat depth without compensations)
  - Posterior chain balance (RDLs, good mornings, GHR)
  - Rotator cuff prehab (external rotation, face pulls)
- **When to train around pain**: 2+ consecutive sessions of deteriorating performance = deload or medical referral

### 13.2 Training Programs

#### 8-Phase Year-Long Periodization (Climbing Magazine)

| Phase | Duration | Focus | Key Sessions |
|-------|----------|-------|--------------|
| Conditioning | 6 weeks | General fitness base | Cardio, core, basic strength, light climbing |
| Low-intensity endurance | 6 weeks | High-volume, low-intensity | ARC training, volume bouldering, mileage |

#### Daily Undulating Periodization (DUP) for Year-Round Climbing

Rather than sequential block periodization, DUP varies focus within each week:

| Day | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| Monday | Max strength/power: limit bouldering, max hangs |
| Tuesday | Endurance: ARC or moderate routes 30–45 min |
| Wednesday | Rest |
| Thursday | Limit bouldering or campus board |
| Friday | Power endurance: 4×4s or route interval |
| Saturday | Outdoor projecting or volume climbing |
| Sunday | Active recovery: light cardio, stretching |

**Zone 2 cardio**: 1–2 hours/week supports recovery between sessions

### 13.3 Training Methods

**ARC Training** (Aerobic Restoration and Capillarization):
- Continuous climbing at 30–40% max intensity
- 20–60 min per session, on auto-belay or traversing
- Improves capillary density, local endurance, technique

**Hangboard Protocols**:
- **Max hangs**: 7–10 sec hangs on smallest edge you can hold with added weight. Full rest (3+ min) between attempts. 5–8 attempts per session.
- **Repeaters**: 7 sec hang / 3 sec rest × 6–8 reps. Multiple sets. Targets anaerobic endurance.

**4×4 Bouldering** (power endurance):
- 4 boulder problems (near max) in a row without rest
- Rest 4 min between rounds
- Repeat 4 rounds
- Total: ~16 problems per session, 2×/week max

### 13.4 Nutrition

| Training Type | Pre-Session | During (if >2 hours) | Post-Session |
|--------------|-------------|---------------------|--------------|
| Limit bouldering | Light carbs 30–60 min before | Water/lighter options | 3:1 carb:protein ratio |
| Endurance/power endurance | Moderate carbs 1–2 hours before | 30–60 g CHO/hour | Replenish within 30 min |
| Outdoor all-day | Carb-rich breakfast | 150–300 kcal/hour, bars, gels | Substantial meal |

- **Forearm recovery**: omega-3s for inflammation, tart cherry juice, adequate protein
- **Hydration**: 500–750 mL/hour climbing, more in hot conditions

### 13.5 Injury Prevention

- **Most common**: finger pulley (A2/A4), elbow medial epicondylitis (golfer's elbow), shoulder impingement, lumbrical tears
- **Prevention priorities**:
  - Finger loading: gradual progression, avoid campus board when cold, proper warm-up
  - Pulley protection: progressive hangboard loading, no dynamic moves on small crimps
  - Shoulder: rotator cuff + scapular stability, contrast external rotation with heavy pulling
  - Elbow: eccentric wrist flexor work (rice bucket, Tyler twist), contrast with extension exercises
- **Key principle**: muscles adapt faster than tendons — tendon remodeling requires 48–72+ hours, take rest days

### 13.6 Recovery

- **Between hard sessions**: minimum 48 hours for finger connective tissue recovery
- **Between climbing days**: at least 1 rest day after limit bouldering
- **Deload**: 1 week every 4–6 weeks (reduce volume 40–50%, maintain moderate intensity)
- **Between seasons**: 1–2 weeks with reduced climbing volume

## 14. Rowing

### 14.1 Key Physical Demands

| Event | Duration | Energy System | Key Metrics |
|-------|----------|---------------|-------------|
| 2000m | 5.5–8 min | Aerobic (77–83%) + Anaerobic (17–23%) | VO2max, power at lactate threshold, stroke efficiency |
| 500m (sprint) | 1.2–2 min | Anaerobic dominant (60%+) | Peak power, rate of force development |
| 5K+/endurance | 18+ min | Aerobic (90%+) | Lactate threshold, fat oxidation |
| Lightweight | Same distances | Same but at lower absolute weight | Higher relative VO2max, weight management |

### 14.2 Training Programs

#### Annual Periodization (World Rowing / FISA Model)

| Period | Duration | Primary Focus | Training Characteristics |
|--------|----------|---------------|--------------------------|
| Preparation 1 | Oct–Dec (2.5–5 mo) | Max strength + general endurance | High volume 14–20 hrs/wk, strength block, aerobic base |
| Preparation 2 | Jan–Feb | General endurance + muscular endurance | Volume remains high, increasing intensity, weight endurance |
| Pre-competition | Mar–Apr | Basic specific endurance + technique | Shift to rowing-specific training, threshold work |
| Competition 1 | May–Jul | Specific endurance, race preparation | High intensity, lower volume, weekly racing |
| Competition 2 | Aug–Sep | Peak for championships | Taper, maintain, peak performance |
| Active recovery | 2–4 weeks | Recovery + light conditioning | Low intensity, varied activities |

**Training Zones for Rowing** (intensity at blood lactate):

| Zone | [La-] mmol/L | % of total training | Training Effect |
|------|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| UT2 (U2) | <2 | ~50–70% in prep phase | Aerobic base, fat oxidation, capillary density |
| UT1 (U1) | 2–3 | ~15–25% | Cross threshold, moderate endurance |
| AT/TR | 3–4 | ~10–15% | Threshold improvement, buffering capacity |
| Transport (T) | 4–6 | ~5–10% | VO2max, specific endurance, |
| Race pace | 6–10 | <5% | Anaerobic capacity, lactate tolerance |

**Pyramidal TID is most common**: progressive reduction from Z1 > Z2 > Z3. As competition approaches, TID becomes more **polarized**.

#### Sample Elite Preparation Week (20+ hours)

| Day | AM | PM |
|-----|-----|-----|
| Monday | OTW: 16km steady UT2 | Strength: max strength focus |
| Tuesday | Erg: 4×15 min UT1 (4 min rest) | Core + flexibility |
| Wednesday | OTW: technique + 20×500m AT/TR | Strength: power emphasis |
| Thursday | OTW: 20km UT2 steady state | Recovery/stretching |
| Friday | Erg: 6×6 min TR (3 min rest) | Strength: max strength |
| Saturday | OTW: 2–3 hours UT2 long distance | — |
| Sunday | Long erg or XT: 90–120 min UT2 | Active recovery |

### 14.3 Strength Training for Rowing

| Phase | Focus | Sets × Reps | Load | Frequency |
|-------|-------|-------------|------|-----------|
| Anatomical adaptation | General conditioning | 3–4 × 8–12 | 40–60% 1RM | 2–3×/week |
| Max strength | Strength development | 3–5 × 4–6 | 75–90% 1RM | 2–3×/week |
| Strength maintenance | Power maintenance | 3–4 × 5–8 | 70–85% 1RM | 2×/week in-season |
| Power conversion | Speed-strength | 3–5 × 3–5 | 60–75% 1RM | 2×/week (pre-competition) |

**Elite GB Rowing Team practice**: ~20% of total training time allocated to strength work (matching anaerobic energy contribution to the 2000m race)

**Key lifting exercises**: deadlift, squat, bench pull, bent-over row, leg press, pull-ups, core circuits

### 14.4 Nutrition

| Phase | CHO | Protein | Calories | Notes |
|-------|-----|---------|----------|-------|
| High volume (prep) | 7–10 g/kg | 1.6–2.0 g/kg | 3500–5500+ kcal/day | Glycogen replenishment critical |
| Competition | 6–8 g/kg | 1.6–2.0 g/kg | 3000–4000 kcal | Weight management for boat |
| Lightweight | 5–7 g/kg (periodized) | 1.8–2.2 g/kg | Controlled | Gradual pre-season loss, not crash |

- **Between double sessions**: immediate CHO + protein for glycogen restoration
- **On-water fueling for long UT2 sessions**: practice race nutrition timing
- **Lightweight considerations**: body fat must be low — periodized low CHO only in consultation with sports nutritionist

### 14.5 Injury Prevention

- **Most common**: low back pain (most frequent), rib stress fracture, patellar tendinopathy (catch), shoulder impingement
- **Prevention priorities**:
  - Low back: core stability, trunk endurance, hip mobility, graduated erg volume
  - Rib: monitor excessive erg volume increases, strengthen serratus anterior and obliques
  - Hamstring flexibility: tight hamstrings associated with low back pain in rowers
- **Ergometer risk**: previous back injury + high erg volume = strong predictor of recurrence; use OTW rowing when possible

### 14.6 Recovery

- **Between sessions**: appropriate for double training days; aerobic sessions can follow each other, but follow anaerobic/lactic with aerobic (lactate clearance)
- **Deload**: reduced training every 3rd–4th week, 3–7 day unloading cycles in competition period
- **Recuperation methods**: massage, contrast baths, sauna, mineral salt baths
- **Sleep**: 9+ hours for elite/development athletes during high-volume periods
- **Nutrition timing**: within 30 min post-session, emphasis on glycogen restoration

## General Principles Across All Sports

### Periodization Comparison

| Model | Best For | Structure | Limitation |
|-------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Linear** | Beginners, simple annual peaks | Steady progression: high vol→high int | Single peak, may detrain other qualities |
| **Block** | Intermediate–elite, multi-sport prep | 3–5 week focused blocks with distinct goals | Requires precise planning |
| **Undulating (DUP)** | In-season, multi-quality demands | Vary intensity/volume within each week | Complex to program manually |
| **Concurrent** | Athletes who need all qualities year-round | Maintain multiple qualities with varied emphasis | May limit peak potential |
| **ATR** (accumulation → transmutation → realization) | Combat, weightlifting, short prep cycles | 3 distinct blocks, aggressive focus per block | High training stress |

### Autoregulation Methods

| Method | How It Works | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| RPE (Reps in Reserve) | Rate perceived difficulty 1–10 | Any strength sport, experienced lifters |
| Velocity-based | Bar speed at fixed load signals readiness | Powerlifting, weightlifting |
| CMJ monitoring | Jump height indicates CNS state | All sports during peaking |
| Heart rate variability | Autonomic recovery status | Endurance + mixed sports |
| Wellness questionnaire | Daily 1–10 readiness self-scoring | Any sport for load adjustment |

### General Taper Guidelines (Bosquet et al. 2007 meta-analysis)

| Variable | Recommendation | Evidence |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| Duration | 8–14 days (2 weeks optimal) | Strong |
| Volume reduction | 41–60% exponential | Strong |
| Intensity | Maintain (do not reduce) | Strong |
| Frequency | Maintain or reduce ≤20% | Moderate |
| Performance improvement | 3–8% above pre-taper | Strong |
