# Periodization — Programming & Planning

## 1. Periodization Definition

### What is Periodization?
Systematic planning of athletic training involving progressive cycling of various aspects of a training program during a specific period.

### Why Periodize?
- Prevent overtraining
- Optimize performance
- Peak at right time
- Avoid plateaus
- Manage fatigue

## 2. Types of Periodization

### Linear Periodization
- **Structure**: Gradual increase in intensity, decrease in volume
- **Example**: 
  - Weeks 1-4: High volume, low intensity (12-15 reps)
  - Weeks 5-8: Moderate volume, moderate intensity (8-12 reps)
  - Weeks 9-12: Low volume, high intensity (4-6 reps)
- **Best for**: Beginners, strength sports
- **Pros**: Simple, effective, proven
- **Cons**: Less flexibility, may not suit everyone

### Undulating Periodization (DUP)
- **Structure**: Vary intensity/volume within week
- **Example**:
  - Monday: Heavy (5x5)
  - Wednesday: Moderate (3x10)
  - Friday: Light (3x15)
- **Best for**: Intermediate/advanced, variety seekers
- **Pros**: More flexible, prevents boredom
- **Cons**: More complex, requires planning

### Block Periodization
- **Structure**: Concentrate specific training blocks
- **Example**:
  - Block 1 (3 weeks): Hypertrophy
  - Block 2 (3 weeks): Strength
  - Block 3 (3 weeks): Power
  - Block 4 (1 week): Deload
- **Best for**: Athletes, advanced trainees
- **Pros**: Focused adaptation, peaks performance
- **Cons**: Requires planning, less variety

### Concurrent Periodization
- **Structure**: Train multiple qualities simultaneously
- **Example**: Strength + endurance in same week
- **Best for**: General fitness, team sports
- **Pros**: Balanced development
- **Cons**: May limit maximum development in each area

## 3. Training Phases

### Phase 1: Anatomical Adaptation (2-3 weeks)
- **Goal**: Prepare joints, tendons, ligaments
- **Volume**: High (3 sets of 12-15)
- **Intensity**: Low (50-65% 1RM)
- **Exercises**: Machines, bodyweight, light weights
- **Frequency**: 3 days/week

### Phase 2: Hypertrophy (4-6 weeks)
- **Goal**: Increase muscle size
- **Volume**: High (3-4 sets of 8-12)
- **Intensity**: Moderate (65-80% 1RM)
- **Exercises**: Compound + isolation
- **Frequency**: 4-5 days/week

### Phase 3: Strength (4-6 weeks)
- **Goal**: Increase maximal strength
- **Volume**: Moderate (3-5 sets of 3-6)
- **Intensity**: High (80-90% 1RM)
- **Exercises**: Primarily compound
- **Frequency**: 3-4 days/week

### Phase 4: Power (3-4 weeks)
- **Goal**: Increase explosive strength
- **Volume**: Low (3-5 sets of 1-3)
- **Intensity**: Very high (90-100% 1RM)
- **Exercises**: Olympic lifts, plyometrics
- **Frequency**: 3 days/week

### Phase 5: Deload (1 week)
- **Goal**: Recovery, adaptation
- **Volume**: Very low (40-60% reduction)
- **Intensity**: Low-moderate
- **Exercises**: Light versions of main lifts
- **Activities**: Mobility, cardio, skill work

## 4. Training Variables

### Volume
- **Definition**: Total work performed (sets × reps × weight)
- **Manipulation**: Increase gradually (10% per week max)
- **Monitoring**: Track total tonnage

### Intensity
- **Definition**: Load relative to 1RM (%)
- **Manipulation**: Increase as volume decreases
- **Monitoring**: RPE, RIR, %1RM

### Frequency
- **Definition**: Sessions per week
- **Manipulation**: Increase with experience
- **Range**: 2-6 days/week

### Density
- **Definition**: Work per unit time
- **Manipulation**: Reduce rest periods
- **Monitoring**: Total session time

### Exercise Selection
- **Compound**: Multi-joint, more muscle mass
- **Isolation**: Single-joint, targeted
- **Order**: Compound first, isolation second

## 5. Program Design

### Full Body (3 days/week)
- **Best for**: Beginners, time-limited
- **Example**: Monday, Wednesday, Friday
- **Exercises**: 5-6 compound movements
- **Volume**: 3-4 sets per exercise

### Upper/Lower (4 days/week)
- **Best for**: Intermediate, balanced development
- **Example**: Monday (Upper), Tuesday (Lower), Thursday (Upper), Friday (Lower)
- **Exercises**: 4-5 per session
- **Volume**: 3-4 sets per exercise

### Push/Pull/Legs (6 days/week)
- **Best for**: Advanced, maximum volume
- **Example**: Push, Pull, Legs, Push, Pull, Legs, Rest
- **Exercises**: 4-6 per session
- **Volume**: 3-5 sets per exercise

### Split Routines
- **Best for**: Advanced, specific goals
- **Example**: Chest/Back, Shoulders/Arms, Legs
- **Exercises**: 3-4 per session
- **Volume**: 4-5 sets per exercise

## 6. Periodization for Specific Goals

### Strength
- **Focus**: Heavy compound lifts
- **Periodization**: Linear or block
- **Key lifts**: Squat, bench, deadlift, overhead press
- **Rep ranges**: 1-6 reps
- **Frequency**: 3-4 days/week

### Hypertrophy
- **Focus**: Volume, time under tension
- **Periodization**: Linear or undulating
- **Key lifts**: Compound + isolation
- **Rep ranges**: 8-12 reps
- **Frequency**: 4-6 days/week

### Endurance
- **Focus**: Cardiovascular training
- **Periodization**: Linear or concurrent
- **Key activities**: Running, cycling, swimming
- **Intensity zones**: Zone 2-5
- **Frequency**: 4-6 days/week

### Power
- **Focus**: Explosive movements
- **Periodization**: Block or concurrent
- **Key lifts**: Olympic lifts, plyometrics
- **Rep ranges**: 1-5 reps
- **Frequency**: 3-4 days/week

## 7. Monitoring & Adjustments

### Tracking Progress
- **Strength**: 1RM tests, rep maxes
- **Hypertrophy**: Measurements, photos
- **Endurance**: Timed tests, heart rate
- **Power**: Vertical jump, sprint times

### Adjusting Programs
- **Plateaus**: Change exercises, rep ranges, or volume
- **Fatigue**: Reduce volume, add deload
- **Injuries**: Modify exercises, reduce intensity
- **Goals change**: Reassess periodization

### Signs to Adjust
- Performance decrease
- Persistent soreness
- Loss of motivation
- Sleep disturbance
- Elevated resting heart rate

## 8. Common Periodization Mistakes

### Mistake 1: No Plan
- **Problem**: Random training, no progress
- **Solution**: Follow structured program

### Mistake 2: Too Much Too Soon
- **Problem**: Overtraining, injuries
- **Solution**: Progressive overload, deloads

### Mistake 3: No Deloads
- **Problem**: Fatigue accumulation
- **Solution**: Plan deloads every 4-6 weeks

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Recovery
- **Problem**: Decreased performance
- **Solution**: Prioritize sleep, nutrition, stress management

### Mistake 5: Not Tracking
- **Problem**: No data to make decisions
- **Solution**: Log workouts, track metrics

## 9. Sample Programs

### Beginner Strength (12 weeks)
- **Weeks 1-4**: 3x10 @ 60% 1RM
- **Weeks 5-8**: 4x8 @ 70% 1RM
- **Weeks 9-11**: 4x6 @ 80% 1RM
- **Week 12**: Deload

### Intermediate Hypertrophy (8 weeks)
- **Weeks 1-2**: 3x12 @ 65% 1RM
- **Weeks 3-4**: 4x10 @ 70% 1RM
- **Weeks 5-6**: 4x8 @ 75% 1RM
- **Weeks 7-8**: 3x6 @ 80% 1RM

### Advanced Power (6 weeks)
- **Weeks 1-2**: 5x3 @ 85% 1RM
- **Weeks 3-4**: 5x2 @ 90% 1RM
- **Weeks 5-6**: 5x1 @ 95% 1RM
