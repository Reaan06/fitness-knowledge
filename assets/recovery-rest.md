# Recovery & Rest — Complete Guide

## 1. Sleep Optimization

### Sleep Duration
- **Adults**: 7-9 hours per night
- **Athletes**: 8-10 hours per night
- **Teenagers**: 8-10 hours per night
- **Consequences of sleep deprivation**: Reduced performance, impaired recovery, increased injury risk

### Sleep Quality
- **Consistency**: Same sleep/wake time daily
- **Environment**: Cool (18-20°C), dark, quiet
- **Pre-sleep routine**: 30-60 minutes wind-down
- **Screen avoidance**: Blue light affects melatonin

### Sleep Stages
- **Light sleep**: Transition stage
- **Deep sleep**: Physical recovery, growth hormone release
- **REM sleep**: Mental recovery, memory consolidation
- **Sleep cycles**: 90 minutes each, 4-6 per night

### Sleep Hygiene
- **Caffeine**: Avoid after 2 PM
- **Alcohol**: Disrupts sleep quality
- **Heavy meals**: Avoid 2-3 hours before bed
- **Exercise**: Avoid intense workouts 2-3 hours before bed

## 2. Active Recovery

### Definition
Low-intensity movement to enhance recovery without adding stress.

### Benefits
- Increased blood flow
- Reduced muscle soreness
- Improved range of motion
- Mental relaxation

### Examples
- **Walking**: 20-30 minutes
- **Swimming**: Easy laps
- **Cycling**: Light effort
- **Yoga**: Gentle flows
- **Foam rolling**: Self-myofascial release

### When to Use
- Day after intense training
- Rest days
- During deload weeks
- When feeling sore

## 3. Passive Recovery

### Definition
Complete rest without intentional movement.

### When to Use
- After competition
- During illness
- When overtrained
- After extreme exertion

### Benefits
- Full physical recovery
- Mental reset
- Hormonal balance
- Immune system support

## 4. Stretching & Mobility

### Static Stretching
- **Timing**: Post-workout or before bed
- **Duration**: 15-60 seconds per stretch
- **Frequency**: Daily
- **Benefits**: Long-term flexibility, relaxation

### Dynamic Stretching
- **Timing**: Pre-workout
- **Duration**: 10-15 minutes
- **Examples**: Leg swings, arm circles, hip circles
- **Benefits**: Warm-up, range of motion

### PNF Stretching
- **Technique**: Contract-relax
- **Benefits**: Most effective for flexibility
- **Requirements**: Partner or equipment
- **Duration**: 3-5 seconds contraction, 15-30 seconds stretch

### Foam Rolling
- **Purpose**: Myofascial release
- **Technique**: Roll slowly, pause on tender spots
- **Duration**: 1-2 minutes per area
- **Areas**: Quads, hamstrings, IT band, calves, back

## 5. Nutrition for Recovery

### Post-Workout Nutrition
- **Protein**: 20-40g within 2 hours
- **Carbs**: 0.8-1.2g/kg within 2 hours
- **Hydration**: 1.5L per kg lost during exercise

### Anti-Inflammatory Foods
- **Omega-3**: Fatty fish, flaxseed, walnuts
- **Berries**: Blueberries, strawberries, cherries
- **Leafy greens**: Spinach, kale, broccoli
- **Turmeric**: Curcumin (absorb with black pepper)
- **Ginger**: Reduces muscle soreness

### Recovery Supplements
- **Tart cherry juice**: Reduces muscle soreness
- **Magnesium**: Muscle relaxation, sleep
- **Zinc**: Recovery, immune function
- **Vitamin D**: Bone health, recovery

## 6. Hydration for Recovery

### Water Needs
- **Baseline**: 35-40ml per kg body weight
- **Exercise**: +500-1000ml per hour
- **Recovery**: 1.5L per kg lost during exercise

### Electrolytes
- **Sodium**: Lost in sweat, essential for nerve function
- **Potassium**: Muscle contractions, fluid balance
- **Magnesium**: Muscle relaxation, sleep quality

### Signs of Dehydration
- Dark urine
- Headache
- Fatigue
- Dry mouth
- Dizziness

## 7. Deload Weeks

### Definition
Planned reduction in training volume/intensity.

### Frequency
- Every 4-6 weeks
- Or when performance plateaus
- Or when feeling overtrained

### Protocol
- **Volume**: Reduce by 40-60%
- **Intensity**: Reduce by 10-20%
- **Duration**: 1 week
- **Activities**: Light cardio, mobility, skill work

### Benefits
- Physical recovery
- Mental refreshment
- Injury prevention
- Performance improvement

## 8. Stress Management

### Impact on Recovery
- Cortisol increases muscle breakdown
- Impairs sleep quality
- Reduces immune function
- Increases injury risk

### Strategies
- **Meditation**: 10-20 minutes daily
- **Deep breathing**: 4-7-8 technique
- **Nature walks**: 20-30 minutes
- **Social connection**: Quality time
- **Hobbies**: Non-fitness activities

## 9. Injury Prevention

### Warm-up
- **Duration**: 5-10 minutes
- **Content**: Dynamic movements, sport-specific
- **Purpose**: Increase blood flow, neural activation

### Progressive Overload
- **Rule**: Increase volume/intensity gradually
- **Recommendation**: 10% per week maximum
- **Signs of overload**: Persistent soreness, performance decrease

### Form Check
- **Priority**: Quality over quantity
- **Technique**: Film yourself, use mirrors
- **Feedback**: Coach, training partner

### Rest Days
- **Frequency**: 1-2 per week minimum
- **Activities**: Active recovery, mobility
- **Importance**: Adaptation happens during rest

## 10. Monitoring Recovery

### Subjective Measures
- **Energy levels**: Scale of 1-10
- **Motivation**: Scale of 1-10
- **Soreness**: Scale of 1-10
- **Sleep quality**: Scale of 1-10

### Objective Measures
- **Heart rate variability (HRV)**: Higher = better recovery
- **Resting heart rate**: Lower = better fitness
- **Performance metrics**: Strength, endurance, speed
- **Body composition**: Weight, measurements

### Recovery Signals
- **Green**: Good energy, motivated, sore but functional
- **Yellow**: Slight fatigue, moderate soreness, reduced motivation
- **Red**: Exhaustion, severe soreness, no motivation, illness

## 11. Recovery for Specific Populations

### Older Adults (>50 years)
- **Sleep**: May need more, quality decreases
- **Recovery**: Longer between sessions
- **Mobility**: Increased focus on flexibility
- **Nutrition**: Higher protein needs

### Women
- **Menstrual cycle**: May affect recovery
- **Luteal phase**: Increased recovery needs
- **Pregnancy**: Modified training, increased rest
- **Postpartum**: Gradual return to training

### Athletes
- **Competition season**: Increased recovery needs
- **Travel**: Jet lag, schedule disruption
- **High altitude**: Increased recovery demands
- **Heat/cold**: Environmental stress

## 12. Common Recovery Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Skipping Sleep
- **Problem**: Impaired recovery, increased injury risk
- **Solution**: Prioritize 7-9 hours nightly

### Mistake 2: No Deloads
- **Problem**: Overtraining, plateaus
- **Solution**: Plan deloads every 4-6 weeks

### Mistake 3: Poor Nutrition
- **Problem**: Slow recovery, muscle loss
- **Solution**: Adequate protein, carbs, hydration

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Pain
- **Problem**: Injuries become chronic
- **Solution**: Address pain early, consult professional

### Mistake 5: Overtraining
- **Problem**: Burnout, decreased performance
- **Solution**: Listen to body, adjust volume/intensity
