# Equipment Guide — Complete Reference

## 1. Free Weights

### Barbell
- **Standard**: 20kg (45 lbs), 7.2ft long
- **Women's**: 15kg (35 lbs), 6.7ft long
- **Olympic**: 20kg, rotating sleeves
- **Benefits**: Compound movements, maximum load
- **Best for**: Squat, bench, deadlift, overhead press

### Dumbbells
- **Fixed**: 1-100+ lbs
- **Adjustable**: Space-saving, various weights
- **Benefits**: Unilateral training, range of motion
- **Best for**: Isolation, unilateral work, home gym

### Kettlebells
- **Standard**: 4-48 kg (8-108 lbs)
- **Benefits**: Ballistic training, grip strength
- **Best for**: Swings, cleans, Turkish get-ups

### Weight Plates
- **Bumper plates**: Rubber, for Olympic lifts
- **Iron plates**: Traditional, durable
- **Change plates**: Small increments (0.25-2.5 lbs)

## 2. Machines

### Cable Machine
- **Types**: Single, dual, functional trainer
- **Benefits**: Constant tension, versatile
- **Best for**: Isolation, rehabilitation

### Smith Machine
- **Description**: Barbell on fixed rails
- **Benefits**: Safety, isolation
- **Drawbacks**: Fixed path, less stabilizer activation

### Leg Press
- **Types**: 45-degree, horizontal
- **Benefits**: Leg training without balance
- **Drawbacks**: Less functional than squats

### Leg Extension/Curl
- **Benefits**: Isolation of quads/hamstrings
- **Drawbacks**: May stress knee joint

### Chest Press Machine
- **Benefits**: Chest training without balance
- **Drawbacks**: Less stabilizer activation

### Lat Pulldown
- **Benefits**: Back training progression
- **Drawbacks**: Less functional than pull-ups

### Seated Row
- **Benefits**: Back training without balance
- **Drawbacks**: Less functional than barbell rows

## 3. Bodyweight Equipment

### Pull-Up Bar
- **Types**: Doorway, wall-mounted, free-standing
- **Benefits**: Upper body strength, pull movements
- **Exercises**: Pull-ups, chin-ups, hanging leg raises

### Dip Station
- **Types**: Portable, wall-mounted
- **Benefits**: Upper body pushing, triceps
- **Exercises**: Dips, knee raises, L-sits

### Gymnastics Rings
- **Benefits**: Unstable surface, core stability
- **Exercises**: Ring push-ups, rows, muscle-ups

### TRX/Suspension Trainer
- **Benefits**: Bodyweight training, core stability
- **Exercises**: Rows, push-ups, pikes, planks

### Exercise Ball
- **Benefits**: Core training, stability
- **Exercises**: Ball squats, planks, hamstring curls

### BOSU Ball
- **Benefits**: Balance, proprioception
- **Exercises**: Squats, lunges, single-leg work

## 4. Cardio Equipment

### Treadmill
- **Benefits**: Walking, running, incline training
- **Features**: Speed, incline, programs
- **Considerations**: Joint impact, space

### Stationary Bike
- **Benefits**: Low-impact cardio
- **Types**: Upright, recumbent, spin
- **Considerations**: Joint-friendly

### Elliptical
- **Benefits**: Low-impact, full-body
- **Considerations**: Less calorie burn than running

### Rowing Machine
- **Benefits**: Full-body cardio, low-impact
- **Considerations**: Technique important

### Stair climber
- **Benefits**: Lower body cardio, glute emphasis
- **Considerations**: High intensity

### Jump Rope
- **Benefits**: Portable, effective, coordination
- **Considerations**: Joint impact, learning curve

## 5. Resistance Bands

- **Loop bands**: For squats, glute activation
- **Tube bands**: With handles, versatile
- **Flat bands**: For rehabilitation
- **Mini bands**: For glute activation

### Resistance Levels
- **Light**: Rehab, activation
- **Medium**: Assistance, light resistance
- **Heavy**: Resistance training
- **Extra heavy**: Advanced training

### Uses
- **Warm-up**: Activation exercises
- **Assistance**: Pull-up progression
- **Resistance**: Squats, rows, presses
- **Rehabilitation**: Gentle resistance

## 9. Equipment Selection Guide

### By Goal
- **Strength**: Barbell, rack, bench
- **Hypertrophy**: Dumbbells, cables, machines
- **Cardio**: Treadmill, bike, rower
- **Functional**: Kettlebells, TRX, rings

### By Space
- **Apartment**: Bands, dumbbells, pull-up bar
- **Garage**: Rack, barbell, bench
- **Commercial**: Full equipment selection

### By Budget
- **Low**: Bodyweight, bands
- **Medium**: Dumbbells, kettlebells
- **High**: Full home gym

### By Experience
- **Beginner**: Machines, dumbbells
- **Intermediate**: Barbell, free weights
- **Advanced**: Specialty equipment

## 10. Equipment Maintenance

### Free Weights
- **Clean**: Wipe after use
- **Inspect**: Check for damage
- **Store**: Properly, not on barbell

### Machines
- **Lubricate**: Moving parts regularly
- **Inspect**: Cables, pulleys, bolts
- **Clean**: Upholstery, frames

### Cardio Equipment
- **Clean**: Regular wiping
- **Maintain**: Follow manufacturer schedule
- **Inspect**: Belts, electronics

### Safety Tips
- **Inspect**: Before each use
- **Clean**: After each use
- **Store**: Properly
- **Replace**: When worn or damaged

## 11. Common Equipment Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Buying Too Much
- **Problem**: Unnecessary equipment, wasted money
- **Solution**: Start minimal, add as needed

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Quality
- **Problem**: Cheap equipment breaks
- **Solution**: Invest in quality basics

### Mistake 3: Wrong Size
- **Problem**: Equipment doesn't fit or work
- **Solution**: Measure space, check specifications

### Mistake 4: No Maintenance
- **Problem**: Equipment breaks down
- **Solution**: Regular maintenance schedule

### Mistake 5: Safety Ignored
- **Problem**: Injuries from equipment
- **Solution**: Proper use, regular inspection
